<h2>Borders, Nationalisms, Identities: The Ethics of Global Citizenship </h2>



The fall 2019 Muhlenberg College Center for Ethics‚ programming includes lectures, a film and a gallery exhibition.

<p style="color:#4D4D4D;font-size:small;">




  



<span style="text-size:small;">	&nbsp;Wednesday, September 11, 2019  00:25 PM</span></p>

<!-- FULL WIDTH Image -->
<img src="https://www.muhlenberg.edu//media/contentassets/images/about/cfe/CFE-BordersNationalismsIdentities-web.png" style="width:100%;" alt="News Image"><br/><span style="color:#4D4D4D;font-size:small;font-style:italic;"></span><br/><br/>


<p>This year‚s Center for Ethics program examines our global moment as controversies arise from a resurgence in nationalism, the simultaneous hardening and permeability of national borders and boundaries and increased flows of people as tourists, refugees, asylum seekers, laborers and students. Traffic in and exchange of raw materials, information, weapons and diseases have increasingly transformed national identities, cultures and conceptions of citizenship.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The program will explore related questions, including: What are the ethical implications these issues present? How should we understand and respond to global changes in borders, nationalisms and identities? Are there historical precedents for such dynamic changes to the lives of peoples, states and our planet? What roles do new technology and communication play in crossing some borders while raising other barriers? How can we imagine our place in national and global societies when we are at the same time brought closer together and driven further apart?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The fall program kicked off with a September 3 campus community event, "</span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Exit West</span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚An Invitation to the Conversation." Faculty panelists Jim Bloom, Ioanna Chatzidimitriou and Sharon Albert presented reflections on Mohsin Hamid‚s novel, </span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Exit West</span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;">, followed by an opening discussion of the Center for Ethics theme via key themes, moments, characters and symbols presented in Hamid‚s work.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><strong>The </strong><a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/aboutus/cfe/upcomingevents/"><strong>fall programming</strong></a><strong>, which includes a number of lectures, a film, a bus trip to Ellis Island and a gallery exhibition, takes place September through November. Additional events will be held during the spring semester. <em>With the exception of the College-only October 27 trip to Ellis Island,</em> a</strong><strong><em>ll events are free and open to the public.</em></strong></p>
<p><strong>Fall 2019 Schedule:</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"><a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/news/2019/fall2019martinartgalleryexhibitions.html"><strong><img src="/media/contentassets/images/academics/Ronny_9071_LR-sm (1)-230x230.jpg" alt="" style="  padding : 5px;   float : left;" />Ronny Quevedo ‚ Art Gallery Exhibit &amp; Talk</strong><br></a></span>September 18‚November 2, 2019<br>Martin Art Gallery and Trexler Library Field</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>September 18, 2019 (5:30‚7 p.m.) Exhibit Opening Reception</strong><br></span>Marten Art Gallery, Baker Center for the Arts</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>October 15, 2019 (7‚8 p.m.) Artist Talk</strong><br></span>Recital Hall, Baker Center for the Arts</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Part of Muhlenberg's Center for Ethics 2019-2020 programming, Quevedo's multi-site exhibition locates the viewer as a player in an imaginary and unsettling space. The works on view, both in the Martin Art Gallery and on the Trexler Library Field, look at a space in negotiation and in constant shift. Acting as a metaphor for shifting landscapes and the role of play in re-imagining one‚s position in the world, </span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Space of Play, Play of Space</span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;"> is an investigation of the self through the games we play by magnifying the role of migration within that transformation.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Quevedo's field-sized outdoor drawing will include fragmented diagrams of basketball, soccer, volleyball and handball courts. Having moved from Ecuador to New York City in the 1980s, Quevedo‚s work interlaces autobiographical and sociological insights in a reflection on his bi-cultural upbringing and his father‚s soccer career as a player and a referee in both places. Taken together, the installation renders the disquiet, yet poetic and exuberant, state of peoples and cultures in global flux through a materially syncretized and conceptually complex body of work.</span></p>
<hr>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span><strong>Borders &amp; Immigration ‚ Current Challenges and Constitutional Concerns<br></strong>Friday, September 20, 2019 (2:00 ‚ 3:45 p.m.)<br>Miller Forum, Moyer Hall</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This Constitution Day panel discussion will feature Theresa Cardinal Brown, director of immigration and cross-border policy for the Bipartisan Policy Center, and pending final details, one additional guest.&nbsp; The panel will be moderated by a member of the political science department and discuss a range of important policy concerns affecting immigration in the contemporary American political context.&nbsp;</span></p>
<hr>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span><strong>Ellis Island Trip (<em>Campus community only; Advanced sign up required</em>)<br></strong>Sunday, October 27, 2019<br><em>Sign up directly with Maureen Bayraktar, Political Science Suite, 3rd Floor, Ettinger Hall, M-F, 9:30 a.m.<span>‚</span>3 p.m.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</em></p>
<hr>
<p><strong>Ieva Jusionyte: ‚Injury and Rescue on the U.S.-Mexico Border‚<br></strong>Wednesday, October 23, 2019 (7‚8:45 p.m.)<br>Miller Forum, Moyer Hall</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Ieva Jusionyte is assistant professor of anthropology and social studies at Harvard University. Her research and teaching interests lie at the intersection of political-legal and medical anthropology, with a focus on the study of state power and the materiality of violence; law and criminalized livelihoods; discourses and infrastructures of security; technologies of injury; politics and ethics of representation; and ethnography as method and storytelling.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Based on fieldwork in the tri-border area between Argentina, Brazil and Paraguay from 2008 to 2014, her first book,</span><em><a href="https://www.ucpress.edu/book/9780520286474/savage-frontier"><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Savage Frontier: Making News and Security on the Argentine Border</span></a></em><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (University of California Press 2015), examines how local journalists both participate in and contest global and national security discourses and practices in a region portrayed as the hub of drug and human trafficking, contraband and money laundering. Drawing on her professional background as a news reporter and experience of producing an investigative television program ‚Proximidad‚ in Argentina, the book probes politics and ethics of representation and knowledge production in ethnography and in journalism. In addition to the book, her work on the tri-border area has appeared in </span><em><a href="https://anthrosource.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/ca.30.1.07"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Cultural Anthropology</span></a></em><span style="font-weight: 400;">, <em>American Ethnologist</em>, <em>Anthropological Quarterly</em>, and <em>Political and Legal Anthropology Review</em>.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Her second research project, supported by the National Science Foundation and the Wenner-Gren Foundation, focuses on security infrastructures and emergency services along the border between Sonora and Arizona. Her new book, </span><em><a href="https://www.ucpress.edu/book.php?isbn=9780520297180"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Threshold: Emergency Responders on the U.S.-Mexico Border</span></a></em><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (University of California Press, 2018), delves into the lives of first responders under heightened security on both sides of the wall.</span></p>
<hr>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span><strong>Amy-Jill Levine : "On Different Grounds: Jewish and Christian Foundation for Engaging the Middle East"<br></strong>Thursday, October 24, 2019<br><em>Location and Details to Follow</em></p>
<p><span>Amy-Jill&nbsp;</span>Levine is a university professor of New Testament and Jewish studies, Mary Jane Werthan Professor of Jewish Studies and professor of New Testament studies at Vanderbilt Divinity School and College of Arts and Science. She is an internationally prominent public speaker on Christian-Jewish relations, an instructor in the Great Courses series and a consultant to the Vatican and the World Council of Churches. Her books include <em>The Misunderstood Jew: The Church and the Scandal of the Jewish Jesus</em> and <em>Short Stories by Jesus: The Enigmatic Parables of a Controversial Rabbi</em>. She is the co-editor, with Marc Z. Brettler, of the <em>Jewish Annotated New Testament</em>.&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This is an affiliated event co-sponsored with the Institute for Jewish Christian Understanding.</span></p>
<hr>
<p><strong><em><img src="/media/contentassets/images/academics/MF18-STILL-Lifeboat-460x258.jpg" alt="" style="  padding : 5px;   float : left;" />Lifeboat</em> ‚ Screening and Discussion with Skye Fitzgerald<br></strong>Monday, December 2, 2019 (7-9 p.m.)<br>Lithgow Science Auditorium, Trumbower 130</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Volunteers from a German non-profit risk the waves of the Mediterranean to pluck refugees from sinking rafts pushing off from Libya in the middle of the night. <em>Lifeboat</em> puts a human face on one of the world's greatest contemporary global crises and provides a spark of hope surrounding how civil society can intervene in the refugee crisis in a meaningful way.</span></p>
