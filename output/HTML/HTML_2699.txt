Walther</strong><br> Lecturer<br> Biology<br> New Science Building, 003<br> Phone: 484-664-4058<br> <a href="mailto:dwalther@muhlenberg.edu">dwalther@muhlenberg.edu</a><a href="mailto:edwards@muhlenberg.edu"><br></a></p>
<p><strong>Education</strong><br> B. S. Biological Sciences, University of Delaware<br> Ph.D. Genetics, University of Wisconsin-Madison<br> Postdoctoral Fellow, Brandeis University</p>
<p><strong>Courses Taught</strong><br> Concepts of Biology: From DNA to Cancer (BIO 107)<br> Principles of Biology II Laboratory (BIO 151)<br> Principles of Biology III Laboratory (BIO 152)</p>
<p><strong>Research Interests</strong></p>
<p class="MsoNormal">My research interests are focused on understanding the mechanism of recombinational DNA repair using yeast as a model organism. I use chromosome tagging and fluorescence microscopy of living yeast cells to study the dynamics and nuclear arrangement of chromosomal loci involved in recombination. I am interested in understanding changes in chromosome dynamics in response to DNA damage through the development and application of new methods in fluorescence microscopy.</p>
