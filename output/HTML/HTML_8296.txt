        <p><strong>Teaching Interests</strong></p>
  <p><div>I teach a variety of statistics courses at both the undergraduate and graduate levels.&nbsp; In all of the courses I teach, my goal is to provide students&nbsp;with skills that are transferable far beyond the classroom setting!&nbsp; In particular, I aim to help guide students in marketing specific skills they learn in my courses.&nbsp; Many students have gone on to use skills they learn in my classes in their research with other professors at Muhlenberg and within their current jobs.&nbsp;&nbsp;</div>
<div></div></p><hr/>


<p><strong>Research, Scholarship or Creative/Artistic Interests</strong></p><p><div>One of my favorite aspects of statistics is that it can be applied to any field of study. And I love learning and finding ways of applying statistics to new areas! I thoroughly enjoy working with students (and fellow faculty) on research or projects they are passionate about.&nbsp;&nbsp;</div>
<div></div>
<div>Some of my CUE projects with students include:&nbsp;analysis of what makes an effective tweet, neurological effects of visual stimuli in flies, ideal conditions for raising isopods in a lab, characteristics of police forces that affect crime rates, the impact of wasted food in Muhlenberg‚s dining hall, and the effect of climate change on when flowers bloom.</div>
<div></div>
<div>In addition to working with students and faculty on collaborative projects, I also thoroughly enjoy many aspects of probability. I gave a presentation in 2019 on the game PIG, helped students prepare for the Actuarial Exam P, and worked with a student on diminishing urn models. I have a keen interest in data visualization, and enjoy learning, coding, and applying visualizations any time I can!! A major focus of my own research&nbsp;involves statistics education:&nbsp; how to clearly communicate concepts, how students learn, and how assessment can be used to&nbsp;clarify expectations &amp; motivate students.</div>
