        <p><span><strong>After 1 year of service - A<strong>vailable to employees in positions budgeted for at least 1,248 hours per year</strong>:</strong></span></p>
<p>The Collegeâs short-term disability plan provides for replacement of all or part of income due to an employeeâs temporary disability stemming from a non-work related illness or injury.</p>
<p>In order to receive short-term disability benefits, an eligible employee must apply for leave under the Family &amp; Medical Leave Act (FMLA) by contacting the Office of Human Resources. Short-term disability benefits will be paid on approved claims during the employeeâs FMLA leave. All short-term disability absences require a physicianâs certification, submittal of a leave application, and physicianâs clearance to return to work.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Full-time employees receive 10 days of short-term disability upon hire and accrue five days at the start of each additional year of service, up to a maximum of 26 weeks (130 days). Note: This accrual is pro-rated to reflect annual budgeted hours for those working less than 2,080 hours per year or on an hourly basis.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Short-term disability benefits commence on the sixth business day for approved claims (employees are required to use sick leave, if available, for the first five days.)&nbsp; Based on the duration of an approved, physician-certified absence, salary is paid at 100% of the budgeted base salary during a short-term disability absence for the number of working days (or hours) that have been accumulated in the employeeâs short-term disability leave bank. Once accumulated short-term disability time has been exhausted, the College will continue short-term disability pay at 60% of the individual's current base salary, up to a maximum of $4,000 per month (whichever is less). Short-term disability pay may not exceed 26 weeks. &nbsp;The College reserves the right to request periodic updates to the physicianâs certification of disability.</p>
<p>Benefits (health insurance, dental insurance, life insurance, retirement contributions, etc.) may be continued during a short-term disability absence as long as the employee remains eligible under the terms of the benefit plan document. The College will continue to make the regular employer contribution for benefits as if the employee were in active service during any month in which leave is being used under the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) and in which the College is continuing salary payments under the terms of the short-term disability plan. Employee contributions equal to those paid by active employees will be withheld from short-term disability checks issued by the College, while employees on unpaid FMLA will need to remit payment monthly in the amount equal to the normal payroll deduction for benefits they elect to continue.</p>

<p><strong>I.&nbsp;</strong><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>STAFF ASSOCIATES &amp; SERVICE PERSONNEL</strong></span>: Non-exempt Staff paid on a biweekly basis earn vacation, as follows:</p>
<p><em>CURRENT VACATION ACCRUAL POLICY - EXPIRING 6/30/22:</em></p>
<ul>
<li>Less than&nbsp;4&nbsp;years of service: 10 days per year</li>
<li>4 - 8 years of service: 15 days per year</li>
<li>9+ years of service: 20 days per year</li>
</ul>
<p><em>NEW VACATION ACCRUAL POLICY - EFFECTIVE 7/1/22:</em></p>
<ul>
<li>Less than 5&nbsp;years of service: 15 days per year</li>
<li>5+&nbsp;years of service: 22&nbsp;days per year</li>
</ul>
<p>The amount of vacation charged is based on&nbsp;the staff member's average work day in accordance with the position's annual&nbsp;budgeted hours. For example:</p>
<ul>
<li>A part-time staff member who normally works&nbsp;4 hours a day in a position budgeted for 1000 hours per year, would charge 4&nbsp;hours to vacation. (50% of the full-time equivalent)&nbsp;</li>
<li><span><span>A&nbsp;full-time staff member who normally works 7 hours a day in a position budgeted for&nbsp;1820 hours per year, would charge&nbsp;7 hours per day to vacation. (87.5% of the full-time equivalent)</span></span></li>
<li>A&nbsp;full-time staff member who normally works 8 hours a day in a position budgeted for 2080 hours per year, would charge 8 hours per day to vacation. (100% full-time)</li>
</ul>
<p><span>As always, requests for vacation leave require your managerâs pre- approval.</span></p>
<p>Accumulation of vacation leave for staff associates and service personnel runs from July 1 of one year through June 30 of the next year. Carryover each July 1 is capped at the amount of vacation time earned in the preceding 12 months plus 10 additional days. For example, a staff member earning 15&nbsp;days of vacation per year may carry-over a maximum of 25 days&nbsp;vacation into the next year.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>II. <span style="text-decoration: underline;">EXEMPT STAFF</span></strong>: Exempt Staff paid on a monthly basis earn vacation each year from September 1 through August 31, up to a maximum of 22 days per year (pro-rated for those working less than 2,080 hours per year). A maximum of fifteen&nbsp;vacation days may be carried forward into the next year.</p>
<p>As always, requests for vacation leave require your managerâs pre- approval.</p>
