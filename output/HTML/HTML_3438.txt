<h2>SGA Committee Representation</h2>
  <p>The work of student government is done largely in committee. In addition to five internal standing committees, the SGA is responsible for placing students on several institutional and faculty committees to represent the student viewpoint.</p>
<p>If you have an interest in serving on an institutional or faculty committee or are a faculty member or administrator seeking student representation, please contact the SGA Executive Secretary.</p>
<p>Grace Gault - 2020-2021 SGA Executive Secretary</p>
<p>Browse the information below to learn more about our committees.</p>
<hr>
<h3><strong>INTERNAL COMMITTEES</strong></h3>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Finance</strong></span></p>
<p>Student Clubs and Organizations can request funds for their activities through the Finance Committee. The committee is also responsible for monitoring all financial transactions related to the expenditure of student fees provided to it for distribution by the College.&nbsp;<span>The Finance Committee shall recommend budgets and special funding for clubs and organizations, develop rules and policies for the distribution of funds, and investigate opportunities to fund special projects.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Standards</strong></span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The Student Government Standards Committeeis tasked with upholding the standards of the Student Government Association. This includes but not limited to: dress code, decorum, internal operations such as attendance, point system, and </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">disciplinary</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> proceedings as outlined by the Bylaws and the Standing Rules.&nbsp;<br><br></span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>DEI</strong></span><br>The Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) committee advocates for the needs of marginalized identities in the Muhlenberg community and beyond through frequent collaboration with students, student groups, departments, offices, faculty, staff, and administration. As it creates guidelines, education opportunities, outreach, and collaborative structural change for all community members, the DEI committee prioritizes listening to voices that are usually not represented in college decision-making while holding college spaces accountable for DEI advances. The DEI committee is also tasked with responding to problematic incidents on campus with action items and structural plans to prevent reoccurring issues from happening and to inspire change beyond one incident.&nbsp; We draft plans based on student input through marginalized identity advisor positions and SGA representatives, widespread surveys, regular conversation, and consistent research on DEI issues.</span></p>
<p><strong><strong>&nbsp;</strong></strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Internally, the DEI committee is in charge of leading DEI education to integrate responsiveness and advocacy more actively into SGA culture. DEI collaborates with the general assembly, the three committees, (Finance, Campus Engagement, and Standards) and institutional committee heads on education and creating programming, ensuring that SGA‚s internal process is representative of marginalized identity groups (through a bifaceted process of increasing representation within SGA while enhancing outreach beyond SGA). Specifically, within the committees, DEI does the following:</span></p>
<p><strong><strong>&nbsp;</strong></strong></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Finance Committee ‚ collaborates in providing financial support and mentorship to marginalized students and student groups while encouraging DEI guidelines and support for all clubs and organizations.&nbsp;</span></li>
</ul>
<p><strong><strong>&nbsp;</strong></strong></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Campus Engagement ‚ collaborate in planning and organizing events and activities and communication (email and social media) regarding DEI specific resources available to students.&nbsp;</span></li>
</ul>
<p><strong><strong>&nbsp;</strong></strong></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Standards ‚ collaborate in implementing new internal policies to SGA such as meeting structure, requirements, education, and other guidelines specific to promoting DEI causes.&nbsp;</span></li>
</ul>
<p><strong><strong>&nbsp;</strong></strong></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Institutional Committees- engages in monthly meetings that include research, discussion, and establishing a tangible framework for institutional committee heads to be DEI advocates</span></li>
</ul>
<p><strong><strong>&nbsp;</strong></strong></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">General Assembly- engage in weekly DEI sharing among members, bring DEI initiatives to general assembly for input and further collaboration between committee members</span></li>
</ul>

<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Ultimately and most importantly, DEI is a wide-reaching committee that is here to listen and work toward positive change that prioritizes the needs of marginalized identities in any facet of the college that needs further attention.<br></span>&nbsp;</p>

<h3><strong>FACULTY COMMITTEES WITH STUDENT REPRESENTATION</strong></h3>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Academic Policy Committee</strong></span></p>
<p class="Normal1">Membership consists of nine voting members: seven elected by the faculty, at least one from each division, and <strong>two students selected by the Student Government Association</strong>. The committee makes recommendations to the faculty about all academic requirements and exercises primary responsibility for long-range planning in academic areas. [<em>Faculty Handbook, Sec. 2.8.1.1, April 2014</em>]</p>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Curriculum Committee</strong></span></p>
<p class="Normal1">Membership consists of nine voting members: seven elected by the faculty, at least one from each division, and <strong>two students selected by the Student Government Association. </strong>The committee exercises jurisdiction over the curriculum, including the review of the entire curriculum, as well as the addition or elimination of courses. [<em>Faculty Handbook, Sec. 2.8.1.2, April 2014</em>]</p>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Faculty Senate Observers</strong></span></p>
<p class="Normal1"><strong>Four students elected by the Student Government Association</strong> from among those students serving on the following standing committees of the faculty: Academic Policy Committee, Curriculum Committee, Library Committee, Teacher Education Committee may attend Faculty Senate meetings. These students have a voice but no vote. [<em>Faculty Handbook, Sec. 2.5.2.a, April 2014</em>]</p>
<p><strong>Writing Program Committee</strong></p>
<p class="Normal1">Membership consists of six elected faculty members, two from each division, plus the Director(s) of the Writing Program and Writing Center, and the Assistant Director of the Writing Center. In order to be elected to the committee, each faculty member must have taught at least one writing-intensive (W) course in the previous two years. In addition, there will be<strong> one student member, to be selected by the Student Government Association, who has been trained as a tutor and served at least once as Writing Assistant to a First-Year Seminar</strong>. The committee reviews and approves new First-Year Seminar and Writing course proposals and special requests for W and FYS credit. [<em>Faculty Handbook, Sec. 2.8.1.9, April 2014</em>]</p>
