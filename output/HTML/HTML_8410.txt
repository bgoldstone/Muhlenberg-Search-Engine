<h2>Leading Environmental Scientist Susan Shaw To Address the Ethics of the Anthropocene at Muhlenberg College
</h2>



Woodrow Wilson Fellow and founder of the Shaw Institute will present a public talk, "Science, Lies and Politics," as part of the College's Center for Ethics Speaker Series on Tuesday, October 23.

<p style="color:#4D4D4D;font-size:small;">




  



<span style="text-size:small;">	&nbsp;Thursday, October 18, 2018  01:44 PM</span></p>

<!-- FULL WIDTH Image -->



<p dir="ltr"><img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/lecturesspeakers/SusanShaw-230x249.jpg" alt="" style="   margin : 8px;  float : left;" />Environmental health scientist and marine toxicologist Susan Shaw,&nbsp;founder and director of the&nbsp;<a href="https://www.shawinstitute.org/">Shaw Institute</a> and professor at the State University of New York, Albany‚s School of Public Health, will visit Muhlenberg College <span>October 22 to 25&nbsp;</span>as a Woodrow Wilson Visiting Fellow. She&nbsp;will deliver&nbsp;a public talk as well as a series of&nbsp;classroom presentations on topics related to key health threats we face in the Anthropocene era as part of the College's&nbsp;<a href="http://icm-tracking.meltwater.com/link.php?DynEngagement=true&amp;H=qJ9juQrYQnz1cqUgIm8psgUQ75z%2Bv%2FUt%2BmzLgDhho6nnEEJMrQdYl2LB7Jnbx8vg%2Btj3KPpkLpr8vQBDoZQxS9qOKVDrH%2BeX%2BpuJR053cirZ%2BH4u2w1N4todEriZRLDgKh4nqq09IBs%3D&amp;G=0&amp;R=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.muhlenberg.edu%2Fmain%2Faboutus%2Fcfe%2Fupcomingevents%2F&amp;I=20181018145112.0000005118a2%40mail6-42-usnbn1&amp;X=MHwxMDQ2NzU4OjViYzg5ZGRkYjJkYjFjZmM3YTE3OTVjNTs%3D&amp;S=D8CRZMKW00gsWHfyDYYpIPu1FESZnu3G0LW7hbx3GvE" target="_blank" data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=http://icm-tracking.meltwater.com/link.php?DynEngagement%3Dtrue%26H%3DqJ9juQrYQnz1cqUgIm8psgUQ75z%252Bv%252FUt%252BmzLgDhho6nnEEJMrQdYl2LB7Jnbx8vg%252Btj3KPpkLpr8vQBDoZQxS9qOKVDrH%252BeX%252BpuJR053cirZ%252BH4u2w1N4todEriZRLDgKh4nqq09IBs%253D%26G%3D0%26R%3Dhttps%253A%252F%252Fwww.muhlenberg.edu%252Fmain%252Faboutus%252Fcfe%252Fupcomingevents%252F%26I%3D20181018145112.0000005118a2%2540mail6-42-usnbn1%26X%3DMHwxMDQ2NzU4OjViYzg5ZGRkYjJkYjFjZmM3YTE3OTVjNTs%253D%26S%3DD8CRZMKW00gsWHfyDYYpIPu1FESZnu3G0LW7hbx3GvE&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1539960748432000&amp;usg=AFQjCNHN1aEnOjHd9GTcz_6ybhDAArKJCQ">Center for Ethics Speaker Series</a>.&nbsp;</p>
<p dir="ltr">Shaw's public presentation,&nbsp;‚Science, Lies and Politics,‚ will take place 7 p.m.,&nbsp;Tuesday, October 23, in the Miller Forum, Moyer Hall. The talk is free and no tickets are required.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Drawing on a 30-year career in environmental science, she will discuss the raging war on science and its consequences, and says, ‚Science plays a critical role in human survival but politics are driving us to planetary ecocide.‚ First delivered at the 2017 TEDxMidAtlantic SUPERPOWERS event in DC,&nbsp;Shaw's presentation points to positive trends ahead, including a revolution in renewable energy across all sectors. The question is, can we get there in time?</p>
<p dir="ltr">‚I appreciate this opportunity to address the Muhlenberg community and raise awareness about the alarming consequences of the fossil fuel age, the Anthropocene,‚ Shaw said. ‚The planet is warming at breakneck speed, our oceans are choking with plastics, and every person on Earth has hundreds of untested chemicals in their body. It is vital for everyone to understand the ethical dimensions of our actions and the broader implications they will have for humankind.‚</p>
<p dir="ltr">The eight classroom lectures Shaw will give throughout the week will cover a variety of topics related to her research and expertise. Subjects will include the hazards of photochemicals, chemical exposure, plastics and human health, the aftermath of the Gulf of Mexico oil spill, and occupational cancer in the fire service. She'll be addressing students in the disciplines of&nbsp;art, biology, chemistry, media &amp; communication, political science and public health<em>.</em></p>
<p>Shaw's visit is sponsored by the dean of academic life and Muhlenberg College‚s Center for Ethics, which selected as its fall 2018 theme,&nbsp;<a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/aboutus/cfe/upcomingevents/">The Ethics of the Anthropocene: Crisis Earth.</a> A program of the Council of Independent Colleges,<span>&nbsp;</span><a href="http://icm-tracking.meltwater.com/link.php?DynEngagement=true&amp;H=qJ9juQrYQnz1cqUgIm8psgUQ75z%2Bv%2FUt%2BmzLgDhho6nnEEJMrQdYl2LB7Jnbx8vg%2Btj3KPpkLpr8vQBDoZQxS9qOKVDrH%2BeX%2BpuJR053cirZ%2BH4u2w1N4todEriZRLDgKh4nqq09IBs%3D&amp;G=0&amp;R=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.cic.edu%2Fmember-services%2Fwoodrow-wilson-visiting-fellows&amp;I=20181018145112.0000005118a2%40mail6-42-usnbn1&amp;X=MHwxMDQ2NzU4OjViYzg5ZGRkYjJkYjFjZmM3YTE3OTVjNTs%3D&amp;S=mMhR9RMJtFggsYgu9qfh5UwbjcI-TUCccwCg6jPK568" target="_blank" data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=http://icm-tracking.meltwater.com/link.php?DynEngagement%3Dtrue%26H%3DqJ9juQrYQnz1cqUgIm8psgUQ75z%252Bv%252FUt%252BmzLgDhho6nnEEJMrQdYl2LB7Jnbx8vg%252Btj3KPpkLpr8vQBDoZQxS9qOKVDrH%252BeX%252BpuJR053cirZ%252BH4u2w1N4todEriZRLDgKh4nqq09IBs%253D%26G%3D0%26R%3Dhttps%253A%252F%252Fwww.cic.edu%252Fmember-services%252Fwoodrow-wilson-visiting-fellows%26I%3D20181018145112.0000005118a2%2540mail6-42-usnbn1%26X%3DMHwxMDQ2NzU4OjViYzg5ZGRkYjJkYjFjZmM3YTE3OTVjNTs%253D%26S%3DmMhR9RMJtFggsYgu9qfh5UwbjcI-TUCccwCg6jPK568&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1539960748432000&amp;usg=AFQjCNEpuEOsOSZixmnrQFyo2x88PJYvyQ">Woodrow Wilson Visiting Fellows</a>&nbsp;has, for more than 45 years, brought prominent scientists, artists, diplomats, journalists, business leaders and other professionals to campuses across the U.S. for substantive dialogue with students and faculty members.</p>
<p dir="ltr"><strong>About Susan Shaw<br></strong>An environmental health scientist, marine toxicologist, explorer, author and passionate ocean advocate, Susan Shaw is widely known for her pioneering research on the toxic legacy of man-made chemicals in the ocean environment. She is credited as the first scientist to show that flame retardant chemicals used in consumer products have contaminated marine mammals and commercially important fish stocks in the northwest Atlantic.</p>
<p dir="ltr">On March 17, 2012, Shaw received the Explorers Club Citation of Merit Award for ‚extraordinary feats of exploration and research‚ and her leadership role in ocean conservation before more than 1,000 people at the Explorers Club Annual Dinner ceremony at the Waldorf-Astoria Hotel, New York City. Shaw chairs The Explorers Club State of the Oceans Forums highlighting solutions to the crisis facing the world‚s oceans.</p>
<p dir="ltr">An outspoken and influential voice on ocean pollution, Shaw dove in the Gulf of Mexico oil slick in May 2010 and has informed the national debate on the hazards of chemical dispersants. She currently leads an investigation on the effects of oil and chemical dispersants in the Gulf ecosystem and serves on the U.S. Department of Interior‚s Strategic Sciences Working Group, a team of scientists charged with assessing consequences of the oil spill and recommending policy actions. She appears in several documentary films on the Gulf disaster including Animal Planet‚s Black Tide: Voices of the Gulf and Green Planet‚s The Big Fix, the Official Selection documentary at the 2011 Cannes Film Festival.</p>
<p dir="ltr">A Fulbright Scholar with dual degrees from Columbia University in film and public health/environmental health sciences, Shaw published Overexposure, the first book on the health hazards of photographic chemicals, in 1983 with Ansel Adams. She is the director and founder of the&nbsp;<a href="https://www.shawinstitute.org/">Shaw Institute</a>&nbsp;and professor at The School of Public Health, Department of Environmental Health Sciences, State University of New York, Albany.</p>
<p dir="ltr">The recipient of numerous awards, Shaw is a Fellow of the Explorers Club, a Woodrow Wilson Visiting Fellow and was named Gulf of Maine ‚Visionary‚ by the Gulf of Maine Council on the Marine Environment.</p>
<p dir="ltr">In 2011 Shaw received the Society of Women Geographers‚ highest award, the Gold Medal, joining the ranks of Amelia Earhart, Margaret Mead, Jane Goodall and Sylvia Earle. She is the 19th woman to receive the Society‚s Gold Medal in 78 years.</p>
