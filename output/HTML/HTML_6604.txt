<p align="left"><strong><span class="style20"><a href="/continuingstudies/prospectivestudents/theyellowribbonprogram/">The Yellow Ribbon Program</a>&nbsp;at Muhlenberg College.<img src="/media/contentassets/images/gce/Yellow_ribbon_001.png" alt="Wescoe School" style="  padding : 2;   float : right;" /><br><br></span></strong>â<span class="style20">Under the&nbsp;<strong><a href="/continuingstudies/prospectivestudents/theyellowribbonprogram/yellowribbongieducationenhancementprogram/">Yellow Ribbon GI Education Enhancement Program</a></strong>&nbsp;tuition and fees are fully covered for veterans with 100% eligibility under the GI Bill<span class="style5">Â®</span>.</span></p>
<p align="left">Muhlenberg College has partnered with the US Department of Veterans Affairs in offering this scholarship which covers the difference between the cost of tuition and the reimbursed amount provided by the post 9/11 GI Bill<span>Â®</span>, thereby covering 100% of the tuition for eligible veterans.</p>

<p align="left"><span class="style20"><strong>We are excited to help you navigate the process to explore majors, understand your benefits, register for classes each semester, and be available for advising throughout your entire time with us.</strong></span></p>
<p align="left">&nbsp;<strong>We suggest the following steps:</strong></p>
<div class="content-add10">
<p class="style7">1). Contact the <strong>School of Continuing Studies</strong>&nbsp;office to make a day or evening appointment with one of our academic advisors either in person or virtually via Zoom: 484-664-3300 or&nbsp;<span>&nbsp;</span><a href="mailto:continuingstudies@muhlenberg.edu" target="_blank">conti<wbr>nuingstudies@muhlenberg.edu</a><span>&nbsp;.</span></p>
2). Bring a copy of any prior coursework or training along to our meeting.&nbsp; We will review it and show how it will apply to your degree path.&nbsp;
<p class="style8">3).&nbsp;Together we will plan an academic and/or career path for you.</p>
</div>
<div class="content-add10">4).&nbsp;Complete the application for the Post 9/11 GI Bill&reg;.&nbsp;</div>
<div class="content-add10">
<p class="style7">5). The<strong> School of Continuing Studies</strong>&nbsp;provides <strong>tuition deferment</strong>, as well as <strong>book vouchers</strong> to our student veterans. Please see your advisor for assistance.</p>

<p>&nbsp;If you served on Active Duty, you might be eligible for education benefits offered by the Department of Veterans Affairs. For example, the Post-9/11 GI Bill<span>Â®</span> provides financial support for educational and housing expenses to individuals with at least 90 days of aggregate service after September 10, 2001, or individuals discharged with a a service-connected disability after 30 days. You must have received an honorable discharge to be eligible for the Post-9/11 GI Bill<span>Â®</span>.</p>
<p>If you are currently serving in the military, you may be eligible for funding offered through the Department of Defense Tuition Assistance program. Check your eligibility status and the amount for which you qualify with your Service prior to enrolling.&nbsp;</p>
<p>If you are the spouse or child of a service member who is serving on active duty Title 10 orders in the pay grades of E1-E5, O1-O2, or W1-W2, you may be eligible for financial assistance from the Department of Defense for education, training, and/or the occupational license and credentials necessary for a portable career.</p>
<p>If you are the spouse or child of a service member, you may be eligible for transfer of the service member's Post 9/11 GI Bill<span>Â®</span> benefits to you.&nbsp;<br>&nbsp;</p>

<p><strong>What happens if I am deployed while enrolled in school?</strong></p>
<p>Students on leave&nbsp;for military deployment will be readmitted with the same academic status achieved when last attended.&nbsp;Length of absence not to exceed 5 years.&nbsp;<em>(Exceptions being Veterans with dishonorable discharge, bad conduct discharge or court martial offenses).</em></p>
