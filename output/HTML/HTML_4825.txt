        <h2>  Words of Advice for Current Students
</h2>

    <div class="panel-body">
        <h3>Build a strong foundation of knowledge<br><br></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚I was fortunate to take a of a couple of service learning courses (Adolescent Development and Developmental Psychopathology) which helped me put what I was learning into practice. I am grateful for these experiences as they have allowed me to work with various populations.‚ </span></p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>- Kate Weisenseel ‚17<br><br></strong></span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚You are more prepared than you think! You are also able to use the base of knowledge to be successful in various areas whether it's completing clinical therapy, research, school counseling, assessments, and more.‚ </span></p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>- Jessica Gonzalez ‚13<br><br></strong></span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚Psychology and the experiences you have in the major can apply to so many different career paths. When I was an assistant coach, people used to ask me if I felt as though I used my psychology and sport psychology background; my response was that even if I didn't explicitly use my education, it always came out in the foundation of my work.‚ </span></p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">- <strong>A.J. Barnold ‚09<br><br></strong> </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚The skills being learned can be applied to so many different types of professions, and career tracks. The psychology major prepares you for both interpersonal relationship building and analytical skills required to make data driven decisions. Having a psychology background doesn't mean you need to become a psychologist, it is a major that helps build a diverse skill set and thoughtful approach to problem solving.‚ </span></p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">- <strong>Adam Nodiff ‚06<br><br></strong></span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚I think there are many avenues to take with a psychology background. Understanding how people work is extremely important in every profession.‚</span></p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><span style="font-weight: 400;"> - <strong>Allison Giordano ‚12 </strong></span><strong><br></strong></p>

    <div class="panel-body">
        <h3><strong>Explore opportunities in and around the community <br><br></strong></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚Keep an open mind, explore, research with a faculty member, take classes in all the disciplines within psychology. You will use them all.‚</span></p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong> - Lauren Westendorf ‚05</strong><br></span><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚Explore all different ideas, challenge yourself and challenge others.‚ </span></p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><strong>- Douglas Kupferman ‚08</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚I would tell current students to get as involved in the program as they can and seek out opportunities outside of the classroom.‚ </span></p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>- Kate Weisenseel ‚17</strong><br></span><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚Explore any internship opportunities that previous psychology majors have done - it's a great way to see what psychology-related work can look like in the ‚real world‚.‚ </span></p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>- Wendy (Eichler) Morrison ‚05</strong><br></span><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚I would recommend that current students take advantage of the opportunities available to them. It sounds cliche, but Muhlenberg psychology students are very lucky to have small classes, accessible professors, and opportunities to conduct research.‚ </span></p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>- Jackie (MacFarlane) Bookman ‚09</strong><br></span><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚Use this time to explore your interests! Volunteer and do internships and don't be afraid to reach out to a company or someone doing a job you think you might want to pursue. Ask if you can shadow them for a day or even if you can just talk to them about their position and career path. As a student I didn't think anyone I might want to reach out to would agree to this, but I was wrong. Follow your interests and start getting involved in that field.‚ </span></p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>- Victoria Benyo ‚12</strong> </span></p>

    <div class="panel-body">
        <h3><strong>Be open and receptive to change <br><br></strong></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚There is also no rush to figure out exactly what you want to do. Life threw me many curve balls after Muhlenberg, but I wouldn‚t want it any other way.‚ </span></p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><strong>- Jessica Gonzalez ‚13</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚Be open to learning outside the classroom. Some of my experiences studying abroad and volunteering in Allentown were just as valuable as my classroom experience.‚ </span></p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><strong>- Miles Davison ‚08</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚Grades aren't everything!! ...Follow your interests and be open to new ones! If you do readings that spark an interest, or you keep coming back to the same topics, don't be afraid to "nerd out" and learn more about it. Then, ask your advisor/professors about it and see where it takes you.‚ </span></p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">- <strong>Ginelle Wolfe ‚16</strong><br></span><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚It is ok to not know what you want to do after you graduate. Sometimes the best thing to do is identify a particular experience or subject area you liked and learn more about how you can pursue that further. Be sure to keep an open mind as there are many opportunities and resources available that you may not know about.‚ </span></p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>- Laura Winger ‚10</strong> </span></p>

<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚There are a lot of ways to use the research skills you gain in your time at Muhlenberg.‚ </span></p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><strong>- Nashalys Rodriguez ‚13<em><br></em></strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚Take the step of asking faculty member about their research. If it sounds interesting, ask to join their research lab.‚ </span></p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><strong>- Wendy (Eichler) Morrison ‚05<em><br></em></strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚I would also advise students not to underestimate the value of the research components of the major. Even in the legal profession, I have encountered studies that I've had to interpret and use in my work. I could not have done that confidently if I hadn't been so immersed in research at Muhlenberg.‚</span></p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong> - Brianna Vinci ‚15</strong> </span></p>
