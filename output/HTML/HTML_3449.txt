  <h2><strong><span style="text-decoration: underline;">2021 Alumni Volunteer Awards</span>&nbsp;</strong></h2>
<h3><strong>The Office of Annual Giving Award</strong></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">This award is presented to class leaders on behalf of the classes that have secured the largest class gift and greatest percentage of giving participation.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><strong>Diane (Schmidt) Ladley ‚70, P‚01</strong></p>
<p><strong>Jill Stetz-Lewis ‚89</strong><strong>&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p><strong>Ed Davis ‚60</strong></p>
<p><strong>Lee Kreidler ‚59, P‚85, GP‚21</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The classes of 1970, led by Diane (Schmidt) Ladley ‚70, and 1989, led by Jill Stetz-Lewis ‚89, achieved the largest class gift overall and for The Muhlenberg Fund. The classes of 1960, led by Ed Davis ‚60, and 1959, led by Lee Kreidler ‚59, P‚85, achieved greatest class participation.&nbsp; </span><strong><br></strong></p>
<h3><strong>Chester Hill ‚20 Award for Leadership by a Class Fund Chair</strong></h3>
<p>This award is presented to the class fund chairs who provided exemplary service <span style="font-weight: 400;">to the College on behalf of their class, and whose efforts played a critical role in securing financial support for The Muhlenberg Fund.</span></p>
<p><strong>Dawn Eilenberger ‚79</strong></p>
<p><strong>Rudy Favocci ‚79, P‚12&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p><strong>Mitch Goldblatt ‚79</strong></p>
<p>The Class Leadership Committee for the class of 1979 went above and beyond to engage their classmates over the last year. Dawn Eilenberger, Mitch Goldblatt and Rudy Favocci spearheaded multiple virtual class gatherings to keep up momentum and encourage their classmates to give back to the College.&nbsp;</p>
<p><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚I volunteer because I want to ensure that future generations of students have the same opportunity to get a first class education and experience, the same sense of community that I enjoyed during my years at Muhlenberg.‚ </span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Dawn Eilenberger ‚79</span></p>
<p><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚I love Muhlenberg and one of my proudest moments was walking my daughter across the stage as she earned her degree.‚ </span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Rudy Favocci ‚79, P‚12</span></p>
<p><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚Muhlenberg is a special place and by continuing to give back as alumni we make our alma mater even stronger.‚ </span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Mitch Goldblatt ‚79</span></p>
<h3><strong>Young Alumni Volunteer Award</strong></h3>
<p>This award is presented to an alum who made significant contributions of energy, <span style="font-weight: 400;">time and talent, and has demonstrated a commitment to the advancement of </span>Muhlenberg through the engagement of our young alumni.</p>
<p><strong>Rebecca Romano ‚14</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">From serving on the HMMS Committee and Young Alumni Council, chairing her 5th reunion committee, hosting job shadow students and returning to the classroom for Alumni Week, Rebecca has remained actively engaged with Muhlenberg since graduating in 2014.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚</span></em><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">I want current and future generations of Mules to have a rewarding experience like I had, and to have access to every opportunity for academic, professional and personal development, whether on campus or beyond the Red Doors.‚ </span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Rebecca Romano ‚14</span></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;"></span><strong>Reunion Volunteer Award</strong></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This award is presented to the reunion volunteers who have shown great</span>&nbsp;dedication and enthusiasm in planning class activities for reunion.</p>
<p><strong>Tim Birch ‚80&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p><strong>Stu Schnall ‚80, P‚08</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Tim and Stu co-chaired a 40th reunion truly like no other. For the first time, Alumni Weekend went virtual in response to the COVID-19 pandemic. Tim and Stu hosted a virtual gathering that included a live performance and art presentation as well as a game of Muhlenberg trivia.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚</span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">My four years at Muhlenberg brought together a love of learning, a series of lifelong friendships and a sense of community among faculty and students that I wish I could have replicated in the years since our graduation.‚&nbsp; </span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Tim Birch ‚80</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚</span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">I am thrilled to see that Muhlenberg is a better place now than it was in my days and that encourages me to ‚give back‚ and help support its continued success.‚ </span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Stu Schnall ‚80, P‚08</span></p>
<h3><strong>The Muhlenberg Network Volunteer Award</strong></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This award is given to an alum who demonstrates a deep commitment to the&nbsp;</span>advancement of our career services and network initiatives.</p>
<p><strong>Les Polk ‚08</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Les has been an avid supporter of Career Center programming, speaking on career-related panels, posting jobs and internships for current students and offering job shadow and externship opportunities. His commitment has created a robust experience for the students that connects their education to real world experience.</span></p>
<p><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚The need for critical thinking skills, complex analysis of problems and the ability to develop creative solutions is in high demand, and Muhlenberg helps prepare graduates for this type of work.‚ </span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Les Polk ‚08</span></p>
<h3><strong>General Pete Award</strong></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This award is presented to an alum or an organization who recently began their</span>&nbsp;volunteer service to the College, demonstrated success in both quality and quantity of volunteer service and shows promise for continued service.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Kelly Owens-Pisano ‚09</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A new member of the Alumni Board, Kelly supports THAW events, serves on the Young Alumni Council and her reunion committee, participates in career networking and speaking engagements and hosts students for job shadow and career road trips. She participates in Alumni Week and is actively involved with the Institute for Religious and Cultural Understanding.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚</span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">My career was started thanks to the visibility and accessibility of a Berg volunteer, so this is my way to pay it forward!&nbsp; </span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Kelly Owens-Pisano ‚09</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> <br></span></p>
<h3><strong>Volunteer Leadership Service Award</strong></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This award is presented to an alum who demonstrates a significant pattern of volunteer service to the College and is actively engaged as a leader in volunteer work through initiatives such as the Career Center, regional clubs, events, fundraising, affinity groups, reunion and the Alumni Board.</span></p>
<p><strong>Lee Kreidler ‚59, P‚85</strong></p>
