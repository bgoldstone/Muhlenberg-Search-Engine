<h2>Muhlenberg College Hosts Talkback Session with "5B" Filmmakers</h2>



The February 25 event is part of the spring Center for Ethics program âPandemic: Response, Resilience, Reflectionâ 

<p style="color:#4D4D4D;font-size:small;">
By:



  Kristine Yahna Todaro  



<span style="text-size:small;">	&nbsp;Thursday, February 17, 2022  09:31 AM</span></p>

<!-- FULL WIDTH Image -->
<img src="https://www.muhlenberg.edu//media/contentassets/images/about/cfe/CFEPandemicgraphicFINAL-web.png" style="width:100%;" alt="News Image"><br/><span style="color:#4D4D4D;font-size:small;font-style:italic;"></span><br/><br/>


<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In the early 1980s, developing AIDS was a literal death sentence. The award-winning feature-length documentary </span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">5B</span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;"> highlights the first AIDS ward unit in the United States. <br></span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The film tells the inspirational story of everyday heroes, nurses and caregivers who took extraordinary action to comfort, protect and care for their patients. <br></span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Muhlenberg is hosting a virtual talkback session, featuring filmmakers and some of the nurses who helped create the worldâs first Special Care Unit for persons with HIV/AIDS on Friday, February 25, 2â3 p.m. </span><a href="https://muhlenberg.zoom.us/j/93388931941?pwd=OEYyODdWU3Y2NGo3a1ZEVEFRQXJ0dz09"><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">The Zoom link is available here.</span></em></a></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Powerful and profoundly moving, </span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">5B</span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;"> is told through first-person testimony of the nurses and caregivers who built Ward 5B in 1983 at San Francisco General Hospital, their patients, loved ones and staff who volunteered to create care practices based in humanity and holistic well-being during a time of great uncertainty.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The result is an uplifting yet candid and bittersweet monument to a pivotal moment in American history and a celebration of quiet heroes worthy of renewed recognition.</span></p>
<p><strong><em>In advance of the discussion with filmmakers, the film is available on a number of streaming platforms, including </em></strong><a href="https://tubitv.com/movies/552763/5b?start=true"><strong><em>free of charge on tubitv.com</em></strong></a><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></em></p>
<p><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">5B</span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;"> was commissioned by Johnson &amp; Johnson from UM Studios, Highway 61 Films and Saville Productions, with the filmmakers granted complete editorial control over the documentary. Co-directed by Academy-Award nominee Dan Krauss and Oscar winner Paul Haggis, </span><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">5B</span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;"> won the Entertainment Lions Grand Prix and is now part of the Museum of Modern Artâs Permanent Collection.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The presenters for the Muhlenberg event include:</span></p>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong><img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/lecturesspeakers/morrison.png" alt="Cliff Morrison 5B" style="  padding : 5px;   float : left;" />Cliff Morrison, RN, BSN, MSM, MSN, ACRN, FAAN</strong> <br>Morrison developed the role of Clinical AIDS Coordinator and, with the support of the hospital administration, the San Francisco Department of Public Health and the University of California San Francisco, created the worldâs first Special Care Unit (the SCU), for persons with HIV/AIDS, Ward 5B. 5B became globally recognized and studied, ultimately becoming the adopted standard of care around the world. <br><br><strong><img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/lecturesspeakers/moed.png" alt="Alison MoÃ«d 5B" style="  padding : 5px;   float : left;" />Alison MoÃ«d, RN, BSN, MSN</strong><br>MoÃ«d was the nurse manager of the first AIDS Special Care Unit nationwide at San Francisco General Hospital Medical Center (SFGHMC) from 1983-1990. She supervised an all-professional staff to provide care in the earliest days of the epidemic and participated in the development of a prototypical AIDS Nursing Care Plan to address their care needs, informed by the input of patients and their loved ones.<br><br><strong><img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/lecturesspeakers/benton.png" alt="Lynda Benton 5B" style="  padding : 5px;   float : left;" />Lynda Benton, Senior Director, Global Community Impact, Johnson &amp; Johnson</strong><br>Benton leads Johnson &amp; Johnson Nursing, representing the companyâs 125-year commitment to supporting and advocating for the nursing profession. She believes that to improve healthcare, nurses need to be championed and supported for their impact, empowered to raise their voices and implement their ideas and valued as innovative leaders helping to shape the future of healthcare.<br>&nbsp;<br><strong><img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/lecturesspeakers/gaul.png" alt="Brendan Gaul 5B" style="  padding : 5px;   float : left;" />Brendan Gaul, Global Chief Content Officer, Mediabrands / Co-Founder &amp; Global President TRAVERSE32</strong><br>One of the producers of 5B, Gaul is responsible for networking and consolidating Mediabrands' suite of creative and media capabilities around the world. His role includes overseeing the launch of a global content division as well as managing short- and long-form original, branded, performance and campaign content opportunities across Mediabrands internationally. Gaul is also Global President of TRAVERSE32, Mediabrandsâ new development and production studio where he runs development, acquisitions and distribution of original narrative and unscripted content.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<hr>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/aboutus/cfe/upcomingevents/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">The 2021-2022 Center for Ethics program âPandemic: Response, Resilience, Reflection<span>"</span> continues through April.</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This yearâs theme explores some of the ethical questions that arise when we pause to think about the current global pandemic including: What lessons can be learned from the scientific collaboration on COVID-19 treatments and vaccines? From receiving a vaccine to wearing masks, how has the pandemic exposed the limits and obligations of individual and collective ethical behavior? How should we respond to the ways in which the pandemic has had disparate impacts based on race and class? How do we think about the rationing of care when health systems get overwhelmed?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The 2021-2022 program directors are Chrysan Cronin, associate professor of <a href="/academics/public-health/">public health</a>, and Lindsey Nagy, associate professor of <a href="/academics/abe/economics/">economics</a>. The director of the Center for Ethics is Brian Mello, professor of <a href="/academics/polisci/">political science</a>.</span></p>
