Students enrolled for 3 or more course units, having a term GPA of at least 3.500 with no grades of D, F, U, or VF for the semester will be eligible for Dean‚s List. Students with an incomplete grade will not be eligible for inclusion on the Dean‚s List until the work is completed and a final grade is recorded.</p>
    </div>

  <a data-toggle="collapse"

     aria-controls="182">
  	<div class="panel-heading" 
       role="tab" 
       id="18">
      <p class="panel-title pull-left">
          Are Continuing Studies students eligible for academic honors?
  </p>
      <p class="panel-title pull-right">
        
            <span class='fa fa-plus-square-o' ></span>
      </p>	
       


      <div class="clearfix"></div>
  	</div>
  </a>
  <div id="182" 
       class="panel-collapse collapse" 
       role="tabpanel" 
       aria-labelledby="18">
    <div class="panel-body">
        <p>For&nbsp;<span>Continuing Studies</span> students seeking a liberal arts degree (B.A. or B.S.), please refer to the ‚Graduation Honors‚ section of the Muhlenberg College Catalog for information regarding honors distinction at graduation.</p>
<p>Students pursuing accelerated degrees through the <span>School of Continuing Studies</span>&nbsp;will receive a ‚<em>With Distinction</em>" designation if they have a<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>3.700 grade point average (GPA)</strong>or above.&nbsp;The GPA is calculated&nbsp;on the courses within the accelerated degree program only, not other general academic requirements (GAR's) they may have taken or transferred.&nbsp;"<em>With Distinction</em>" will be noted on their transcript and diploma.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div><div class="panel panel-default">
  <a data-toggle="collapse" 
     data-parent="#collapse-accordion"

     aria-controls="192">
  	<div class="panel-heading" 
       role="tab" 
       id="19">
      <p class="panel-title pull-left">
          How many courses may I transfer to Muhlenberg and how do I obtain transcripts from previous academic institutions?
  </p>
      <p class="panel-title pull-right">
        
            <span class='fa fa-plus-square-o' ></span>
      </p>	
       


      <div class="clearfix"></div>
  	</div>
  </a>
  <div id="192" 
       class="panel-collapse collapse" 
       role="tabpanel" 
       aria-labelledby="19">
    <div class="panel-body">
        <p>You may transfer up to 16 courses taken at accredited colleges and universities; these courses can count towards a Muhlenberg College degree. Muhlenberg College requires that an official, sealed transcript of all transfer coursework be sent directly to the <span>School of Continuing Studies</span>&nbsp;from previous academic institutions for review.</p>
<p>Most colleges/universities have on-line transcript requisition, typically available on the Registrar website.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div><div class="panel panel-default">
  <a data-toggle="collapse" 
     data-parent="#collapse-accordion"

     aria-controls="202">
  	<div class="panel-heading" 
       role="tab" 
       id="20">
      <p class="panel-title pull-left">
          Besides taking classes, what other ways does the School of Continuing Studies recognize academic performance for credit?
  </p>
      <p class="panel-title pull-right">
        
            <span class='fa fa-plus-square-o' ></span>
      </p>	
       


      <div class="clearfix"></div>
  	</div>
  </a>
  <div id="202" 
       class="panel-collapse collapse" 
       role="tabpanel" 
       aria-labelledby="20">
    <div class="panel-body">
        <p>There are other ways students may obtain credit for prior learning. The following are different ways the&nbsp;<span>School of Continuing Studies</span> measures academic performance:</p>
<p><strong>CLEP testing</strong>:&nbsp; Students can demonstrate their knowledge and earn credit for prior learning by taking a CLEP exam (College-Level Examination Program) in the subjects already familiar to them. The test is administered by appointment in the <span>School of Continuing Studies</span>&nbsp;office on a computer directly linked to College Board CLEP.&nbsp; Please call office at 484-664-3300 for more information or visit<span style="text-decoration: underline;"><a href="http://www.collegeboard.com/clep" target="_blank">www.collegeboard.com/clep</a></span></p>
<p><em>Military Experience</em><em>:</em>&nbsp; Certain military experience can be assessed for credit in accordance with the guidelines established by ACE (American Council on Education). Your academic advisor will need a copy of your VA DD214 form, as well as any other military record documentation, such as your<span>&nbsp;</span><a href="https://www.goarmyed.com/public/public_programs_services-aarts_transcripts.aspx" target="_blank">AARTS</a><span>&nbsp;</span>or<span>&nbsp;</span><a href="https://jst.doded.mil/smart/signIn.do" target="_blank">SMART</a><span>&nbsp;</span>transcript, stating specific dates, duration, qualification and location of your training. Visit the&nbsp;<a href="/continuingstudies/prospectivestudents/veteransportal/">Veteran's Portal</a>&nbsp;for more information.</p>
<p><em>Prior Work/Career Training Experience</em><em>:</em>&nbsp; The <span>School of Continuing Studies</span>&nbsp;assesses work experience on a case-by-case basis. Adult learners bring a lifetime of learning from their workplace to the classroom. Much of this experience can be directly applied to college-level credit. The American Council on Education (ACE) recommends baccalaureate credit for career‚focused education provided by certain companies, societies, associations, and institutions. We call this training ‚Credit for Prior Learning.‚</p>
<p>It is advantageous for you to speak to your academic advisor about any career training you have received and/or career examinations that you have passed. Ultimately, your advisor will need specific documentation from the company administering such training to determine its college credit-worthiness. Specifically, you will need to obtain:</p>
<ol>
<li>- The number of hours you attended</li>
<li>- Course description about the type of training/examination</li>
<li>- Certificate or proof of achievement written by the administering company</li>
<li>- Information about whether or not you had to take an examination to receive the certificate of achievement.</li>
</ol>
