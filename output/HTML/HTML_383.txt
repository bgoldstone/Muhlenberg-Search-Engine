<h2>Muhlenberg Hosts 2022 Pennsylvania U.S. Senate Democratic Debate</h2>



The first debate for the Pennsylvania Democratic candidates was broadcast and held on campus in front of a live audience on April 3.

<p style="color:#4D4D4D;font-size:small;">
By:



  Kristine Yahna Todaro  



<span style="text-size:small;">	&nbsp;Thursday, April 7, 2022  02:50 PM</span></p>

<!-- FULL WIDTH Image -->
<img src="https://www.muhlenberg.edu//media/contentassets/images/newscenter/academics/Debate stage.jpg" style="width:100%;" alt="News Image"><br/><span style="color:#4D4D4D;font-size:small;font-style:italic;">From left: Candidates Pennsylvania State Representative Malcolm Kenyatta and United States Representative Conor Lamb on the debate stage with moderators Ari Mittleman, Jenny DeHuff and Christopher Borick.  Images: Marco Calderon/Muhlenberg College</span><br/><br/>


<p>An enthusiastic crowd filled the Seegers Union Event Space on the afternoon of Sunday, April 3, for the first 2022 Pennsylvanian <span>U.S. Senate Democratic debate.</span></p>
<p>Democratic candidates State <span>Representative&nbsp;</span>Malcolm Kenyatta and <span>U.S. Representative&nbsp;</span>Conor Lamb addressed a variety of issues, answering questions about the challenges of political polarization, their support of unions,&nbsp;the rising costs facing the state's&nbsp;farmers, the impact of opioids, their stance on climate and energy issues as well as the economic and foreign policies they support.</p>
<p>The debate organizers were informed on March 31, without explanation, that Pennsylvania Lieutenant Governor&nbsp;John Fetterman<span><span>&nbsp;</span>would not be attending the debate.</span></p>
<p><span><img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/academics/Debate - AJ.jpg" alt="" style="     " /></span></p>
<p><em><sub>AJ Henley '24, president of the Muhlenberg College Democrats, opened the debate.</sub></em></p>
<p><span>Pennsylvanians will<span>&nbsp;go to the polls on Tuesday, May 17, to choose their nominee for the open Senate seat vacated by&nbsp;retiring Republican Senator&nbsp;Pat Toomey.</span></span></p>
<p><span>The debate&nbsp;was held in front of a live audience and broadcast by&nbsp;</span>PCN.<span>&nbsp;</span><a href="https://pcntv.com/election/" target="_blank"><strong>The broadcast is available to view without a paywall on&nbsp;PCN's Election 2022 webpage</strong></a>,&nbsp;under 'Democratic Race for U.S. Senate.'</p>
<p><img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/academics/Debate & student row.jpg" alt="" style="     " /></p>
<p>The debate was co-sponsored by&nbsp;<a href="https://www.cityandstatepa.com/" target="_blank" data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.cityandstatepa.com/&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1647120419678000&amp;usg=AOvVaw0IpwyZ8luoT_tA5WslHL7n">City &amp; State PA</a>,&nbsp;<a href="https://www.papoliticspodcast.org/" target="_blank" data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.papoliticspodcast.org/&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1647120419678000&amp;usg=AOvVaw31dUFj290Y_QiiObxY1pOR">Pennsylvania Kitchen Table Politics</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;the&nbsp;<a href="https://muhlenberg.edu/aboutus/polling/" target="_blank" data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://muhlenberg.edu/aboutus/polling/&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1647120419678000&amp;usg=AOvVaw3o9oJ6oxh3egTQlLi0Qcxi">Muhlenberg College Institute of Public Opinion</a>&nbsp;as well as by the student organizations Muhlenberg College Democrats and <em>The Muhlenberg Weekly.</em></p>
<p>AJ Henley '24, president of the College Democrats, opened the debate by reminding the audience that the war in Ukraine is a powerful reminder that democracy and the ability to cast an educated vote should never be taken for granted.</p>
<p>"The opportunity to gather here today<strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">â</span></strong>as candidates face questions and address voters without preconditions<strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">â</span></strong>is a fundamental aspect of freedom of expression and a hallmark of our democracy," they added.</p>
<p><img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/academics/Debate - Lamb & students.jpg" alt="" style="     " /></p>
<p><em><sub>U.S. Representative&nbsp;Conor Lamb greets students following the debate.</sub></em></p>
<p>A dozen Muhlenberg students volunteered for the debate, acting as guides for&nbsp;the candidates and members of the press, checking tickets and welcoming audience members.</p>
<p>The debate was moderated by Christopher Borick, Professor of&nbsp;<a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/academics/polisci/" target="_blank" data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.muhlenberg.edu/academics/polisci/&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1647120419678000&amp;usg=AOvVaw1EdgSolSFLgoiFY0nPHVfe">Political Science</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;director of the Muhlenberg College Institute of Public Opinion; Jenny DeHuff, City &amp; State PA editor-in-chief; and Ari Mittleman, founder and host of PA Political Podcast: Pennsylvania Kitchen Table Politics.</p>
<p><img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/academics/Debate - Kenyatta & students.jpg" alt="" style="     " /></p>
<p><em><sub>Pennsylvania State Representative Malcolm Kenyatta with students after the debate.</sub></em></p>
<p>Reporters from&nbsp;two dozen national and local media outlets attended the live debate, in some cases&nbsp;<a href="https://twitter.com/daveweigel/status/1510694336270422019?s=20&amp;t=ybEmckH2EktsSI3uIz6y2g" target="_blank">live tweeting throughout</a>&nbsp;the event.&nbsp;<span>To date, news about the debate has appeared in nearly&nbsp;90&nbsp;articles and videos, including&nbsp;<em><a href="https://www.inquirer.com/politics/election/pa-democratic-senate-debate-fetterman-lamb-kenyatta-20220403.html" target="_blank">The Philadelphia Inquirer</a>,</em>&nbsp;</span><a href="https://triblive.com/news/pennsylvania/lamb-kenyatta-face-off-minus-fetterman-in-debate-of-democratic-senate-hopefuls/" target="_blank" data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://triblive.com/news/pennsylvania/lamb-kenyatta-face-off-minus-fetterman-in-debate-of-democratic-senate-hopefuls/&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1649439043785000&amp;usg=AOvVaw3S1U_zfKagsjLiEKJ-Jj8p">Bloomberg News</a><span>,&nbsp;<em><a href="https://www.nationaljournal.com/s/717070/the-star-of-the-show-doesnt-show-at-pennsylvania-senate-debate/?unlock=PFXRRNNQFFOBM268" target="_blank">National Journal</a>,</em> </span><em><a href="https://www.politico.com/newsletters/playbook/2022/04/04/democrats-two-do-confirm-jackson-land-manchin-00022646" target="_blank">POLITICO</a></em>&nbsp;and <span><em><a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2022/04/05/trailer-socialism-beijing-dave-primary-stretch-pennsylvania-senate-contenders-go-after-front-runners/" target="_blank" data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2022/04/05/trailer-socialism-beijing-dave-primary-stretch-pennsylvania-senate-contenders-go-after-front-runners/&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1649439043785000&amp;usg=AOvVaw0L6_igmusT1tZx48V22Zwp">The Washington Post</a></em></span>.</p>
