  <h3><strong>Executive Board Executive Officers</strong></h3>
<h5><strong>Carol Ekizian Papazian ‚79, President</strong></h5>
<p><img src="/media/contentassets/images/alumni/unnamed-1-157x157.jpg" alt="" style="     " /></p>
<p><span>Carol is a member of the Class of 1979 and graduated cum laude with a double major in Humanities (self-designed) and Art. She was a member of the College Choir, the Drama Club, the Cardinal Key Society and Class Council.&nbsp; Carol has served on each of her Reunion Committees and is in her third term on the Alumni Board, serving her second year as president. She is a member of the<span>&nbsp;</span></span><span>Henry Melchior Muhlenberg Society.</span></p>
<p><span><span>Carol received an M.A. in Communication from Temple University and her expertise is in Marketing Communication and Brand Positioning.&nbsp; She has held leadership and executive positions in advertising and marketing functional areas and has worked for Young &amp; Rubicam and for AT&amp;T. Carol holds two US Patents for product innovation.</span></span></p>
<p class=""><span>More recently, Carol extended her marketing leadership into the field of Education. She served as Communications Director for the Readington Township Public Schools. As an Adjunct Professor at Raritan Valley Community College, she taught Public Speaking and Public Relations and initiated and developed Service Learning partnerships with business leaders, trade specialists, career services and the local community. She has been cited for ‚best practices‚ and has conducted faculty workshops and presented in non-profit symposiums. Carol‚s company ‚What Next ‚ Life Strategies for Young Adults.‚ provides mentorship for young adults to market and position themselves for career and personal success. Carol has held volunteer leadership roles in community, civic and religious organizations.</span></p>
<p><span><span><span>Carol and her husband John live in Whitehouse Station, NJ.</span></span></span></p>
<hr>
<p><strong>Michael Yellin '05, Vice President<br></strong></p>
<p><img src="/media/contentassets/images/alumni/unnamed-4-157x157.jpg" alt="" style="     " /></p>
<p>Mike earned his undergraduate degree from Muhlenberg College in 2005,&nbsp;<em>cum laude</em>, with honors in Political Science. While at Muhlenberg, Mike was an active member of the community, serving on the Student Government all four years, including as Student Body President during his senior year. During his tenure at the College, Mike was also a Campus Tour Guide, Resident Advisor, member of the Sigma Phi Epsilon fraternity, and Frisbee golf fanatic.</p>
<p>After graduation, Mike earned his&nbsp;<em>juris doctorate</em>&nbsp;from Seton Hall University School of Law, where he served as Student Bar Association President during his third year. Michael has spent his professional career as an attorney with Cole Schotz P.C., where he was recently named a Partner, focusing his practice on the acquisition and sale of distressed properties and debt, partnership disputes, commercial real estate litigation, project development, asset disposition, foreclosures, and bankruptcy-related workouts and restructuring.</p>
<p>Since joining the Alumni Board in 2015, Mike has served on the Career Services Committee. Mike has also served on his reunion committee and been an active member of the Northern New Jersey Alumni Club.&nbsp;</p>
<hr>
<p><strong>Dawn Everett Lezon ‚87, Secretary<br></strong></p>
<p><img src="/media/contentassets/images/alumni/unnamed-3-157x157.jpg" alt="" style="     " />&nbsp;</p>
<p class=""><span>Dawn is a member of the Class of 1987. She graduated<span>&nbsp;</span></span><span><em>magna cum laude<span>&nbsp;</span></em>with a major in&nbsp;Accounting and a minor in Information Systems. At Muhlenberg, she was a member of the&nbsp;Dance Club, and taught aerobics classes weekly at the fitness center. Dawn also ran a calculus&nbsp;workshop to provide tutoring and studying assistance to students. She is serving her fourth term on the Alumni Board, currently as Secretary, and is a member of the Henry Melchior Muhlenberg Society.</span></p>
<p class=""><span>Dawn is a CPA and received an MBA in Finance from Stern School of Business, NYU. She is currently the Director of Accounting Programs at Monroe College in NY where she has found immense satisfaction sharing her passion for accounting with young, aspiring professionals. Previously she spent 30 years as an accounting professional, most recently as the Head of Finance at The Investment Fund for Foundations, a not-for-profit organization that provides investment services to endowed non-profits.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class=""><span>Dawn and her husband Steven recently moved to Stamford, CT. Their son, Thomas, graduated from Lebanon Valley College and daughter, Rebecca, is currently attending James Madison University.</span></p>
<hr>
<p><strong>Bret Kobler '94, Treasurer<br></strong></p>
<p><strong><img src="/media/contentassets/images/alumni/unnamed-17.jpg" alt="" style="     " /></strong></p>
<p class=""><span>Bret earned his degree from Muhlenberg College in 1994, magna cum laude, with a double major in Business Administration and Spanish.&nbsp; While at Muhlenberg, Bret was inducted into Phi Sigma Iota, the Foreign Language Honor Society, and Omicron Delta Epsilon, the International Honor Society for Economics. He was a Peer Tutor and Freshman Advisor.</span></p>
<p class=""><span>Bret is his Class Fund Chair and has served on each of his reunion committees. He is a member of the&nbsp;Henry Melchior Muhlenberg Society.</span></p>
<p class=""><span>Bret is currently a Corporate Vice President in Learning &amp; Development at Eagle Strategies, LLC, a Registered Investment Adviser and wholly-owned subsidiary of The New York Life Company.</span></p>
<p class=""><span>Bret and his husband, Mike Doyle, also Class of 1994, reside in New York, NY. Mike and Bret married in The Egner Memorial Chapel in 2016.&nbsp; They honored the occasion by establishing The Bret G. Kobler ‚94 and Michael J. Doyle ‚94 Scholarship, a four-year award that will recognize an incoming freshman who has demonstrated commitment to service and inclusion in his or her school and local community.</span></p>

<h3><strong>General Members<br></strong></h3>
<p><strong>Margaret Bernhard '11</strong></p>
<p><img src="/media/contentassets/images/alumni/unnamed-7.jpg" alt="" style="     " />&nbsp;</p>
<p>Maggie&nbsp;Bernhard is a proud Mule, having studied History and Media &amp; Communication. While at Muhlenberg, Maggie spent a lot of time in the Office of Community Engagement. She was President of Alpha Phi Omega, chair of the Jefferson Field Day Planning Committee, a Scholar in Service to Pennsylvania through the AmeriCorps program, and led weekly volunteer projects at The Caring Place. Her senior year, she was awarded the Board of Associates Community Service Award.</p>
<p>Following the path she started on at Muhlenberg, Maggie earned her Master of Public Administration, with a concentration in Non-Profit Management, from Rutgers-Newark. While completing her MPA, Maggie worked in Volunteer Management at Jersey Cares and then New York Cares, both part of the Points of Light Network. At these organizations, Maggie worked to engage corporate and civic groups in volunteer service and train new volunteer leaders.</p>
<p>Maggie currently works at Family Promise, where she has been since 2018. Family Promise is the leading national non-profit addressing the issue of family homelessness. Maggie works directly with 200 Family Promise Affiliates across the country to ensure they have the resources to engage their community and keep children stably housed.</p>
<p>In her spare time, you can find Maggie making nerdy crafts, baking cookies, learning about retro video games from her husband, and listening to True Crime podcasts.</p>

<p><span>Drew and his Wife, Britton, are the Owners/Directors of Camp Watitoh, a resident summer camp located in Becket, MA. Drew is an&nbsp;active member of the American Camp Association, Moderator of the Center Lake District and an American Camp Association Standards visitor.&nbsp;&nbsp;<br></span></p>
<p>Drew and Britton have two children, Shane and Helen, and live in Maplewood, NJ.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<p><img src="/media/contentassets/images/alumni/unnamed-9.jpg" alt="" style="     " /></p>

While on campus, she was a member of the Muhlenberg Dance Association and the Perkulators Dance team, serving on the Executive Board for both organizations. Kaitie also participated in Student Government, Class Council, and was a Tour Guide. During her senior year, she served as a Presidential Assistant for the Theatre &amp; Dance department. </span></p>

<p><span>Since graduating from Muhlenberg, Kaitie has remained an active volunteer for the College. In addition to her role on the Alumni Board, she served on the executive board of the Young Alumni Council. <p><strong>Christina Coviello '02</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;<img src="/media/contentassets/images/alumni/unnamed-10.jpg" alt="" style="     " /></p>
