  <h3 class="intro-add02">Department News</h3>
<p><strong>Students</strong><strong>&nbsp;Inducted Into Sigma Pi Sigma Physics Honor Society</strong></p>
<p>Students Charles Bene '18, Daniel Buckwalter '18, Meredith Jones '19 and Leen Madanat '19, were each inducted into Sigma Pi Sigma, the Physics Honor Society, on May 4th, 2018.</p>
<p align="left">Physics Department members Adam Clark, Judith Parker and Brett Fadem performed the induction ceremony on Muhlenberg College‚s Campus.&nbsp; The society has some 75,000 historical members. Election to Sigma Pi Sigma is a lifetime membership.</p>
<p align="left">Sigma Pi Sigma exists to honor outstanding scholarship in physics; to encourage interest in physics among students at all levels; to promote an attitude of service of its members towards their fellow students, colleagues, and the public; to provide a fellowship of persons who have excelled in physics. Sigma Pi Sigma‚s mission is not completed in the induction ceremony with the recognition of academic accomplishment. In the four dimensions of Honor, Encouragement, Service, and Fellowship, the mission of Sigma Pi Sigma takes a longer view.</p>
<p>For more information concerning Sigma Pi Sigma please see this<span>&nbsp;</span><a href="https://www.sigmapisigma.org/about">link.</a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p><strong>Daniel F. Buckwalter '18 -THE CLIFFORD R. MOYER MEMORIAL PRIZE IN PHYSICS<br></strong><em><span>Awarded to a student majoring in physics who displays exceptional qualities of scholarship,&nbsp;</span></em><em><span>perseverance and commitment typified by Clifford Moyer.</span></em></p>
<p><strong>Kelly Tornetta '19 is Named a 2018-2019 Rossing Physics Scholar</strong></p>
<p><span>A physics major and mathematics minor, Kelly is one of 12 annual award winners nationwide.&nbsp;<a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/news/2018/kellytornetta19isnameda2018-19rossingphysicsscholar.html" target="_blank">Read More</a></span></p>

<p style="text-align: center;"><em>(left to right) Kristina, Kelly, Carly, Ricky, Allen &amp; Carlos</em></p>
<p align="left">Founded in 1921, Sigma Pi Sigma is a member honor society of the Association of College Honor Societies. The society has some 75,000 historical members. Election to Sigma Pi Sigma is a lifetime membership.</p>
<p align="left">Sigma Pi Sigma exists to honor outstanding scholarship in physics; to encourage interest in physics among students at all levels; to promote an attitude of service of its members towards their fellow students, colleagues, and the public; to provide a fellowship of persons who have excelled in physics. Sigma Pi Sigma‚s mission is not completed in the induction ceremony with the recognition of academic accomplishment. In the four dimensions of Honor, Encouragement, Service, and Fellowship, the mission of Sigma Pi Sigma takes a longer view.</p>
<p>For more information concerning Sigma Pi Sigma please see this<a href="http://www.sigmapisigma.org/sigmapisigma" target="_blank">&nbsp;link</a>.</p>

<p style="text-align: left;" align="center"><strong>‚Berg Students Look at the Sun with Help from the Lehigh Valley Amateur Astronomical Society</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: left;" align="center"><strong><img src="/media/contentassets/images/academics/physics/astronomy/image.jpg" alt="" style="   margin : 0px auto; display: block; " /><br></strong></p>
<p>On September 15, 2015 ‚Berg Students had a look at the Sun with the Help from the Lehigh Valley Amateur Astronomical Society.</p>
<p>Sun spots and solar prominences are being viewed through special Solarscopes brought to campus by members of the Lehigh Valley Amateur Astronomical Society (<a href="http://www.lvaas.org">www.lvaas.org</a>) . The telescope with a white light filter allows all wavelengths of light through but reduces the intensity to a safe viewing level so that students can view sunspots on the photosphere of the sun. The Hydrogen alpha scope only allows light at the wavelength of the Hydrogen alpha line at 656.2 nanometers for viewing the flares and prominences visible especially at the limb of the sun.</p>

<p>Students Alexa Carollo'14, Nicole "Nicki" Cronin '14 and Matt Skater&nbsp; '14, were each inducted into Sigma Pi Sigma, the Physics Honor Society, on May 4th, 2014.</p>

<p><strong>‚Berg Physics Majors Have a Blast at the 8<sup>th</sup> Annual Cantalobber Contest</strong></p>
<p>First year physics majors, Carlos Herrera Acevedo, Emran Lallow Victor Lora &amp; Alan Mendez designed, built and entered a pneumatic cantaloupe launching cannon in Kutztown University‚s 8<sup>th</sup> Annual Cantalobber Contest on April 6<sup>th</sup>, 2014. The team‚s air cannon, known as the ‚ Muhlenlobber‚, was entered in the pneumatic division of the contest and the team placed a respectable 3<sup>rd</sup> with an average distance of &nbsp;811 ft, more than two and a half football fields!</p>
<p><strong><img src="/media/contentassets/images/academics/physics/Trophy.jpg" alt="" style="   margin : 0px auto; display: block; " /></strong></p>
<p align="center"><span class="style1">Emran Lallow, Alan Mendex, Victor Lora &amp; Carlos Herrera Acevedo pose with their trophy.</span></p>
<p>The cannon‚s design includes a 10ft long, 6‚ diameter launch tube attached to 14 ft long air tank that has a volume of approximately 35 liters.</p>
<p><img src="/media/contentassets/images/academics/physics/clip_image002_001.jpg" alt="" style="   margin : 0px auto; display: block; " /></p>
<p>The cannon, shown above, is supported all around with a wooden structure. The air tank was pressurized to ~100psi and quickly vented into the launch tube, giving the average cantaloupe a launch velocity of nearly 115 mph.</p>

  <p><strong>The 2013 American Physical Society Division of Nuclear Physics Fall Meeting</strong></p>
<p>Research into the nature of nuclear matter under extreme conditions (and another new topical area) continues for faculty and students at Muhlenberg. Dr. Fadem and five Muhlenberg students (Benjamin Kimelman, Kathryn Kooistra, Julian Rutkowski, MariAnne Skolnik, and Cora Wallace) attended the American Physical Society Division of Nuclear Physics 2013 Fall meeting in Newport News, Virginia. Fadem presented a talk entitled, <em>Transverse Energy at Forward Rapidities at RHIC using the PHENIX Muon Piston Calorimeter</em>.</p>
<p>Four of the students (Kimelman, Kooistra, Rutkowski, and Wallace) presented posters related to the transverse energy measurement. MarriAnne Skolnik presented a poster on research related to the possibility of heavy photons, a hot topic at this year's meeting. Marianne independently applied for and recieved a SULI Fellowship to work at Jefferson Labs.</p>
<p>The posters sported the following titles:</p>
<p><em>Detector response of the PHENIX Muon Piston Calorimeter for sqrt(S_NN) = 200 GeV Au+Au Collisions</em>, by Benjamin Kimelman</p>
<p><em>Comparison of UrQMD, Hijing, and parameterized data as inputs to PHENIX detector simulations of RHIC Au+Au collisions in the forward/backward kinematic region</em>, by Kathryn Kooistra</p>
<p><em>Study of the influence of neutral pion decay on the inflow and outflow of energy from the PHENIX Muon Piston Calorimeter</em>, by Julian Rutkowski</p>
<p><em>&nbsp;</em><em>Calibrating the PHENIX Muon Piston Calorimeter for Au+Au Collisions at Sqrt(S_NN) = 200 GeV</em>, by Cora Wallace</p>
<p>Support for this research included the National Science Foundation Research at Undergraduate Institutions grant to Muhlenberg College (grant number 1209240), and the Muhlenberg College Provost's Office. <em>&nbsp;</em></p>
<p><em>Light Yield Measurements of Heavy Photon Search (HPS) Muon Scintillator Hodoscopes</em>, by MariAnne Skolnik</p>
<p>MariAnne's attendance and presentation at the conference was supported by SULI Fellowship and the Muhlenberg College Provost‚s office.</p>
<p><em>We'll all have to stay tuned to learn whether "dark" photons mediate interactions of the mysterious dark matter as well as the many other open physics topics discussed at the conference!</em></p>

<p><strong>Students</strong><strong>&nbsp;Inducted Into Sigma Pi Sigma, Physics Honor Society</strong></p>

<p>Students Nathan Crossette '13 and Benjamin Schweid '13, were inducted into Sigma Pi Sigma, the Physics Honor Society, on May 3rd, 2013.</p>
<p>Physics Department faculty Adam Clark and Jane Flood performed the induction ceremony at the Hoffman House on Muhlenberg College‚s Campus.</p>
<p align="center"><img src="/media/contentassets/images/academics/physics/sps_induction.JPG" alt="" style="   margin : 0px auto; display: block; " /><span class="style1">Proud students Nate Crossette &amp; Ben Schweid (center) are inducted into Sigma Pi Sigma<br> by Dr. Adam Clark (left) and Dr. Jane Flood (right).</span></p>

<p>Sigma Pi Sigma exists to honor outstanding scholarship in physics; to encourage interest in physics among students at all levels; to promote an attitude of service of its members towards their fellow students, colleagues, and the public; to provide a fellowship of persons who have excelled in physics. Sigma Pi Sigma‚s mission is not completed in the induction ceremony with the recognition of academic accomplishment. In the four dimensions of Honor, Encouragement, Service, and Fellowship, the mission of Sigma Pi Sigma takes a longer view.</p>

<p><strong>Astronomy Students Put the Sun in Clear View</strong></p>
<p>Muhlenberg Physics students in this semester‚s astronomy course had the opportunity to view solar prominences and sun spots thanks to members of the <a href="http://www.lvaas.org">Lehigh Valley Amateur Astronomical Society</a>.&nbsp; Each semester LVAAS members bring telescopes to campus at the invitation of Dr. Judith Parker, Lecturer of Physics, but the solarscopes were a first this semester.&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: center;" align="left"><img src="/media/contentassets/images/academics/physics/sunview1.JPG" alt="" style="   margin : 0px auto; display: block; " /><br> <span class="style1">Members of LVAAS and the Muhlenberg community view the sun.</span></p>
<p align="left"><br> Three tripods were set up in front of the &nbsp;Trumbower Science Building on Muhlenberg‚s campus.&nbsp; Each tripod supported two telescopes.&nbsp; The larger telescope had a ‚white light filter‚ mounted onto the front of the tube.&nbsp; While the filter appeared to be a sheet of ordinary tin foil, it is actually a metal coated polymer material made specifically for solar filters blocking out over 99.999% of the light making it safe for student viewing.&nbsp; Students were able to see sun spots on the sun‚s surface through this scope.&nbsp;&nbsp; The smaller scope is a dedicated solar telescope that provides a ‚hydrogen-alpha light‚ view of the sun.&nbsp; Students were able to observe solar prominences emitted from the sun.<br> &nbsp;</p>
<div>71 students and their guests signed in and more than an equal number of faculty, staff and curious students passing by were eager to check out the sun.</div>

<p><strong>NSF Awards Muhlenberg College a three-year Research at Undergraduate Institutions (NSF-RUI) award of $162,000</strong></p>

<p><strong>Student Presents Poster at the Northeast Conference for Undergraduate Women in Physics</strong></p>
<p>Physics major MariAnne Skolnik attended the <a href="http://ncuwp.org/">Northeast Conference for Undergraduate Women in Physics</a> at Yale University in New Haven, CT. (January 13th -15th, 2012). There she presented her poster entitled: <em>Dark Current in PHENIX Resistive Plate Chambers.</em> The poster describes research work she performed in the summer of 2011 concerning the PHEINX detector at Brookhaven National Laboratory with the guidance of Dr. Brett Fadem.</p>
<p align="center"><img src="/media/contentassets/images/academics/physics/MariAnnePoster_000.JPG" alt="" style="   margin : 0px auto; display: block; " /><span class="style1">MariAnne&nbsp;(second from the left) and friends in front of her poster during the student poster session.</span></p>

<h3>Students <strong>Inducted Into Sigma Pi Sigma, The Physics Honor Society</strong></h3>
<p>Three students were inducted into Sigma Pi Sigma, the Physics Honor Society, on May 6th , 2011. Inducted were Jonathan Ben-Benjamin, Nicole Hammer and Lauren Ryan. The event took place at the <a href="http://www.davinci-center.org/">Da Vinci Science Center</a> in Allentown, PA. Members of the Physics Department faculty and staff performed the induction ceremony. Founded in 1921, Sigma Pi Sigma is a member honor society of the Association of College Honor Societies. The society has some 75,000 historical members. Election to Sigma Pi Sigma is a lifetime membership.</p>
<p class="style1" align="center"><img src="/media/contentassets/images/academics/physics/SPSPHOTO1.JPG" alt="" style="   margin : 0px auto; display: block; " />Sigma Pi Sigma Inductees Lauren Ryan, Jonathan Ben-Benjamin and Nicole Hammer.</p>
<p>Sigma Pi Sigma exists to honor outstanding scholarship in physics; to encourage interest in physics among students at all levels; to promote an attitude of service of its members towards their fellow students, colleagues, and the public; to provide a fellowship of persons who have excelled in physics. Sigma Pi Sigma‚s mission is not completed in the induction ceremony with the recognition of academic accomplishment. In the four dimensions of Honor, Encouragement, Service, and Fellowship, the mission of Sigma Pi Sigma takes a longer view.</p>
<p>For more information concerning Sigma Pi Sigma please see this <a href="https://www.sigmapisigma.org/about">link.</a></p>

<p align="left"><strong>Students Participate in Annual Cantalobber Contest</strong></p>
<p>Muhlenberg Physics students Andrew Nederlof, Ben Schweid, Steve Motchswiller, Nate Crossette, and Kyle Sullivan participated in Kutztown University‚s 5th Annual Catalobber Contest. Students Alex Isinhue and Matt Moscowitz helped build the ‚Berg‚s trebuchet but didn't attend the contest.</p>
<p align="center"><img src="/media/contentassets/images/academics/physics/clip_image002_000.jpg" alt="Physics" style="     " /></p>
<p class="style1" align="center">Ben Schweid, Andrew Nederlof, Nate Crossette and Steve Motschwiller</p>
<p class="style1" align="center">(Clockwise from noon)</p>
<p>The contest was held at Kutztown University on Sunday, April 10th. Seven different teams from five different colleges and universities participated in the event. There were two classes of Cantalobbers, trebuchets and air cannons. Muhlenberg‚s entry was a floating arm trebuchet. They placed fourth in field of four with total summed yardage of 116 yards.&nbsp; The winning trebuchet had a total summed yardage of 660 yards.&nbsp;</p>
