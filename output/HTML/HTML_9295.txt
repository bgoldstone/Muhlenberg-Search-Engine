      <!--<h2>Government Documents Access Policy</h2>-->
      <p><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">This library is a congressionally designated depository for U.S. Government documents. Public access to the government documents collection is guaranteed by public law. (Title 44 United States Code)</span></em></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">As a Federal Depository Library, the public has free access to any government publication in Trexler Library. Most government documents are listed in the Libraryâs online catalog. Most depository documents are shelved in open stacks and are available during the Libraryâs normal service hours. Some documents have been relocated to the Rare Book Room and are available from personnel at the Lending Services Desk on Level A or from our Special Collections Department, located on Level B of the Library. Some print publications do not circulate, most specifically decennial Census materials and legislative titles, i.e. Federal Register, Congressional Record and others. These materials, however, are shelved in the open stacks, freely accessible to the public, but limited to in-library use only. Printer and scanning stations are available, as are microfilm and microfiche readers. Unmediated, electronic access to government publications and information is available by several means:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Full text access to many documents is available from the </span><a href="https://trexler.muhlenberg.edu/library/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Libraryâs online catalog</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">. All online government information may be printed, downloaded, or emailed. Students may use the </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Ricoh/Papercut</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">printers; visitors will be charged&nbsp; $.06 per page for black and white copies, $.16 per page for color copies.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Government publications on CD-ROM are accessible on two specially marked PCs in the Concourse on Level A. These computers are wholly dedicated to accessing government information available on CD-ROMs, on the Internet, or through direct links from the Libraryâs on-line catalog. The CD-ROMs are stored in the two towers next to the PCs.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Free Internet access to government information and publications is available locally or remotely from the </span><a href="http://libraryguides.muhlenberg.edu/content.php?pid=471100&amp;sid=3874373"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Libraryâs documents webpage</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Most hard copy government publications may circulate to the public. The following policies apply:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Government documents may be checked out to anyone with a library card. Faculty and staff may check them out for a semester. Students may check them out for a period of 28 days. All other patrons may check them out for 21 days. A renewal may be possible.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Pennsylvania (local or state) and federal political workers, upon filling out an application, will be issued Town Borrowers cards and may check out government documents for a period of 21 days, with one renewal. The normal registration fee will be waived and the card must be renewed annually.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Town patrons who are not Muhlenberg affiliates, alumni or LVAIC borrowers wishing to check-out materials, including government documents, must register as Town Borrowers and pay an annual registration fee of $25.00.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Requests for exceptions to the above policies are referred to the</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Public Services &amp; Student Engagement Librarian</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> and approved by the Library Director.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Please ask at the Information Services desk if you need help with accessing and locating government documents.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Reviewed 2021</span></em></p>
