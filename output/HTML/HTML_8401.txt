<h2>Elections 2018: The Politics of Urgency and Uncertainty 
</h2>



Nonpartisan lecture and event series at Muhlenberg College addresses the issues surrounding midterm elections.

<p style="color:#4D4D4D;font-size:small;">




  



<span style="text-size:small;">	&nbsp;Tuesday, September 4, 2018  11:45 AM</span></p>

<!-- FULL WIDTH Image -->
<img src="https://www.muhlenberg.edu//media/contentassets/images/academics/polisci/2018-ElectionSeries-w.jpg" style="width:100%;" alt="News Image"><br/><span style="color:#4D4D4D;font-size:small;font-style:italic;"></span><br/><br/>


<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">On November 6, 2018, Americans will elect 435 members of the House of Representatives; 35 U.S. Senators; 39 governors; 6,070 state legislators; and thousands of mayors, city council members, school board members and township commissioners.</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br></span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In a national political context in which politics is anything but usual, few midterm elections have&nbsp;</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">seemed as urgent or as uncertain. Join the Muhlenberg College community for a series of speakers and events,&nbsp;both before and after the&nbsp;midterm elections, that will address the following questions and more:</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">How and to what extent will the election be a referendum on the Trump presidency? What will the elections of 2018 reveal about the current state of American political parties and the ideological polarization between liberals and conservatives? How will issues such as race, immigration, national security, trade, reproductive rights and gun control inform the political debate and shape voters‚ choices at the polls? What forces‚interest groups, money, the media, ideology‚have the great potential to shape electoral outcomes whether in support of or opposition to democracy? How will the outcomes of the 2018 elections matter to national (and international) politics moving forward?</span></p>
<p><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Unless otherwise noted, </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">all Elections 2018 events are free and open to the public.</span></em><span style="font-weight: 400;"> For questions about the program, please contact Lanethea Mathews-Schultz,&nbsp;<span>chair and professor of <a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/polisci/">political science</a>, at&nbsp;<a href="mailto:mathews@muhlenberg.edu">mathews@muhlenberg.edu</a> or 484-664-3737</span></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span><strong><strong>&nbsp;</strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>ALL IN Campus Democracy Challenge<br></strong><strong>Ongoing Fall 2018</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Muhlenberg College has joined hundreds of other colleges and universities for </span><a href="https://www.allinchallenge.org/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Civic Nation‚s ALL IN Campus Democracy Challenge</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, which recognizes campuses that are committed to improving democratic engagement, increasing voter participation rates and graduating informed and active citizens. The Challenge at Muhlenberg links academic departments, offices of student affairs and student organizations, including </span><a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/community-engagement/voterregistration/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚BergVotes</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, in collaborative efforts to increase meaningful campus dialogue, promote voter registration and mobilization and create a more inclusive democracy. The Challenge does not support or oppose candidates for public office or take positions with respect to political parties. Instead, it encourages active and informed democratic participation. For more information about how to get involved, contact Michele Deegan, professor of political science, at </span><a href="mailto:deegan@muhlenberg.edu"><span style="font-weight: 400;">deegan@muhlenberg.edu</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></p>
<p><strong><img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/lecturesspeakers/Goss headshot - tight-230x197.jpeg" alt="" style="   margin : 5px;  float : left;" />Kristin Goss: Whatever Happened to the ‚Missing Movement‚?<br></strong><strong>Gun Control Politics Over Two Decades of Change<br></strong>Thursday, September 13<br>Moyer Hall, Miller Forum, 7 p.m.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Goss‚ work focuses on how everyday Americans participate in public life‚with a particular focus on guns, gender and giving‚and why it matters. The Kevin D. Gorter Associate Professor of Public Policy and Political Science at Duke University, Goss is author of ‚The Paradox of Gender Equality: How American Women‚s Groups Gained and Lost Their Public Voice‚ (University of Michigan Press, 2013) and ‚Disarmed: The Missing Movement for Gun Control in America‚ (Princeton University Press, 2006, 2009) and co-author of ‚The Gun Debate: What Everyone Needs to Know‚ (Oxford University Press, 2014). Before entering academe, she was a Washington-based journalist covering non-profit organizations and foundations for The Chronicle of Philanthropy.</span></p>
<p><strong><img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/charlie v goliath-230x155.png" alt="" style="   margin : 5px; display: block; float : right;" />‚Charlie vs. Goliath‚<br></strong><strong>Film screening with post-film discussion<br></strong>Friday, September 28<br>Seegers Union, Red Door, 2 p.m.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚</span><a href="http://www.charlievsgoliath.com/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Charlies vs. Goliath</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">‚ is a feature-length documentary about an ordinary man‚s extraordinary struggle to shake up the political establishment. It would be hard to find a more unlikely candidate than Charlie Hardy, a penniless, 75-year-old former Catholic priest who spent nearly a decade serving the poor while living in a cardboard shack in a Venezuelan slum. Charlie returned to his hometown of Cheyenne, Wyoming in 2011 and decided to run for office in 2014, winning the Democratic Party‚s nomination for the U.S. Senate. The film provides a portrait of this uniquely charismatic septuagenarian while revealing an inside look at a campaign fueled by hope rather than money. Challenging the belief that our political process is fair and democratic, ‚Charlie vs. Goliath‚ explores the questions of how an honest and sincere person without money can make a difference running for public office, and more broadly, whether it is worth fighting an unbeatable foe and dreaming an impossible dream.</span></p>
<p><strong><img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/lecturesspeakers/davies head shot-150x150.jpg" alt="" style="   margin : 5px;  float : left;" />WHYY‚s Dave Davies: State and Local Political Coverage in Pennsylvania‚Perspectives on a Changing Media Landscape<br></strong>Tuesday, October 9<br>Moyer Hall, Miller Forum, 7 p.m.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Davies has covered state and local politics and government in Philadelphia and Pennsylvania for more than 30 years. He‚s currently senior reporter for the Philadelphia public radio affiliate WHYY-FM and a contributor and fill-in host for ‚Fresh Air with Terry Gross.‚ From 1990 to 2010 he was reporter and columnist for the Philadelphia Daily News. Before joining the Daily News, Davies served as city hall bureau chief for KYW News Radio and city hall correspondent for WHYY.&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><strong><img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/lecturesspeakers/Dardani - Election 2018-230x243.jpg" alt="" style="   margin : 5px;  float : right;" />Ross Dardani: The Least Dangerous Branch? The Myth of the Supreme Court in American Politics<br></strong>Friday, October 12<br>Seegers Union, GQ Annex, 12-2 p.m.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Dardani‚s research and teaching interests include law and society, constitutional law, critical race theory, U.S. government and American political thought. He studies how the Supreme</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Court influences American politics, the manifold ways law matters to ordinary citizens and the societal forces that shape the U.S. legal system. He has a particular focus on how the Supreme Court‚s decisions involving race relations are influenced by the larger structural forces of U.S. society. A visiting assistant professor of political science at Muhlenberg, Dardani earned his B.A. &nbsp;&nbsp;at SUNY New Paltz and his Ph.D. at the University of Connecticut in the fields of public law and political theory.</span></p>
<p><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Open to the public with lunch provided; space is limited. RSVP to Maureen Bayraktar at bayraktar@muhlenberg.edu or 484-664-3066.</span></em></p>
<p><strong>Election Night Party<br></strong>Tuesday, November 6<br>Seegers Union, Red Door, 7 p.m.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Join friends and colleagues for an evening of pizza, live news coverage and election returns as the nation selects its new leaders.</span></p>
<p><strong><img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/lecturesspeakers/63977_christopher-baylor cropped-230x277.jpg" alt="" style="   margin : 5px; display: block; float : left;" />Christopher Baylor: The Origins of Party Transformation<br></strong>Monday, November 26<br>Moyer Hall, Miller Forum, 7 p.m.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Baylor, a political scientist and Muhlenberg College alumnus, is author of ‚First to the Party: The Group Origins of Political Transformation,‚ which explores who controls parties, why and with what kinds of consequences. He received his Ph.D. from the University of California, Los Angeles and his M.A. from Brown University. His work has been published by ‚Studies in American Political Development,‚ ‚Political Science Quarterly,‚ ‚The Washington Post‚ and ‚Fortune.‚ Baylor has taught at Wellesley College and the College of the Holy Cross and has most recently been an American Political Science Association Congressional Fellow.</span></p>
<p><strong><img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/lecturesspeakers/Patrick Deneen-230x287.jpg" alt="" style="   margin : 5px;  float : right;" /></strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Patrick J. Deneen</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">: </span><strong>Why Liberalism Failed‚but Is Conservatism the Answer?<br></strong>Thursday, February 7, 2019<br>Moyer Hall, Miller Forum, 7 p.m.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Deneen is the author of ‚Why Liberalism Failed‚ (Yale University Press, 2018), which has been </span><a href="https://www.patrickjdeneen.com/why-liberalism-failed-reviews"><span style="font-weight: 400;">reviewed by dozens of major media outlets including The New York Times, Washington Post, and Wall Street Journal</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">. Professor of political science and David A. Potenziani Memorial College Chair of Political Science at the University of Notre Dame, Deneen‚s current work focuses on the growing conflict between‚and potential new alignments arising out of‚a globalist meritocratic elite and populist nationalists. His other books include ‚Conserving America? Thoughts on Present Discontents‚ (St. Augustine Press, 2016) and ‚Democratic Faith‚ (Princeton University Press, 2005). Deneen was speechwriter and special advisor to the director of the United States Information Agency (1995-1997) and served as assistant professor of government at Princeton University (1997-2005) and Tsakopoulos-Kounalakis Associate Professor of Government at Georgetown University (2005-2012) before joining the faculty of Notre Dame in 2012.</span></p>
<hr>
<p><strong>Voting at Muhlenberg College</strong></p>
<p><a href="https://goo.gl/maps/tqsmxBgBY882"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Seegers Union</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> at Muhlenberg College is the polling place for the 11th Ward, 4th District in Allentown. Students who reside at Muhlenberg and who are registered to vote in Lehigh County, Pennsylvania will vote at this polling location.</span></p>
<p><strong>Voter Registration</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In order to vote in Pennsylvania (PA), you must register to vote 30 days prior to Election Day. Voter registration information and forms to register in Lehigh County can be obtained from the Office of Voter Registration (</span><a href="https://www.lehighcounty.org/Departments/Voter-Registration"><span style="font-weight: 400;">lehighcounty.org</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">) or the Muhlenberg College </span><a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/main/campuslife/community-engagement/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Office of Community Engagement</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> on the lower level of Seegers Union. If you are a first-time PA voter or voting at a PA polling location for the first time, you may be asked to show identification at the polls. For a list of approved identification forms, visit </span><a href="https://www.votespa.com/Pages/default.aspx"><span style="font-weight: 400;">votespa.com</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">. To register to vote in other locations, contact your local county board of elections, or visit the Muhlenberg Office of Community Engagement for assistance.</span></p>

<p style='font-size:small;font-style:italic;'><strong>About Muhlenberg College </strong><br/>Founded in 1848, Muhlenberg is a highly selective, private, four-year residential, liberal arts college offering baccalaureate and graduate programs. With an enrollment of approximately 2,200 students, Muhlenberg College is dedicated to shaping creative, compassionate, collaborative leaders through rigorous academic programs in the arts, humanities, natural sciences and social sciences; selected preprofessional programs, including accounting, business, education and public health; and progressive workforce-focused post-baccalaureate certificates and master‚s degrees. Located in Allentown, Pennsylvania, approximately 90 miles west of New York City, Muhlenberg is a member of the Centennial Conference, competing in 22 varsity sports. Muhlenberg is affiliated with the Evangelical Lutheran Church in America.</p>
