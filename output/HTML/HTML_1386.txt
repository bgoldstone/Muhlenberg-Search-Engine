  <h3>Course Descriptions&nbsp;</h3>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Recommendations for Study Away from Campus<br></strong> Students are strongly urged, in consultation with their faculty advisor, to plan their major early in their career at Muhlenberg so that space can be provided for important Study Aboard experience and/or out-of-classroom learning such as the Washington Semester and internships.</p>
<p><strong>Recommended Language Courses</strong><br> International studies majors are strongly encouraged to demonstrate competence in a language other than English by majoring or minoring in a language. Alternatively, if schedules do not permit a minor, students are strongly encouraged to take courses in a language through Advanced Conversation &amp; Composition. Students may complete one of the required writing intensive courses with one of the following:<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; FRN 304 Approaches to Textual Analysis 1 course unit<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; RUS 303, 304 Advanced Russian Conversation &amp; Composition 1 course unit (when offered as 304)<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; SPN 304 Advanced Spanish Conversation &amp; Composition 1 course unit</p>
<p>In addition, students may fulfill the general academic requirement in humanities with one of the following courses:<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; RUS 320 Russian Culture &amp; Civilization 1 course unit<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; SPN 320 Civilization of Spain 1 course unit<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; SPN 322 Civilization of Latin America 1 course unit</p>
<p>Finally, international studies majors may find the following courses useful:<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; FRN 310 French for the Professions 1 course unit<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; SPN 310 Business Spanish 1 course unit</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p><strong>Recommended Math and Science courses:<br> </strong>It is strongly recommended that students in International Studies use the following course toward their Reasoning requirement:</p>
<p>&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp;<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; MTH 119 Statistical Analysis 1 course unit</p>
<p>It is strongly recommended that students in International Studies use the following courses to meet their Natural Sciences and Mathematics requirement:<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; BIO 101 Concepts of Biology: Human Biology, Science, &amp; Society 1 course unit<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; BIO 109 Concepts of Biology: Bubonic Plague to AIDS: The Influence of Infectious Disease on the Human Species Environment&nbsp;<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 1 course unit<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; BIO 111 Concepts of Biology: Crisis Earth: Causes, Consequences, &amp; Solutions for a Changing Planet 1 course unit<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; CHM 101 Chemistry of the Environment 1 course unit<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; ESC 111 Topics in Environmental Science 1 course unit<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; ESC 113 Environmental Science I 1 course unit<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; ESC 114 Environmental Science II 1 course unit</p>
<p>&nbsp;<br><em><strong>MAJOR REQUIREMENTS</strong></em><br> The major consists of six core courses plus six courses in either one of the seven designated concentrations or a specialized concentration.</p>
<p><strong>Core Course Requirements:</strong><br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; ECN 101 Principles of Macroeconomics 1 course unit<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; IST 101 Introduction to International Studies 1 course unit<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; PSC 339 or 340 Theories of International Relations 1 course unit</p>
<p><strong>CUE Seminar:</strong><br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; PSC 420 Seminar in Regimes &amp; Regime Changes 1 course unit <strong>OR</strong><br>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; PSC 430 Seminar in Comparative National Security Policy 1 course unit <strong>OR</strong><br>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; PSC 440 Seminar in International Studies 1 course unit</p>
<p><strong>Two (2) Area Studies Courses from the following:<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </strong>HST 293, 294 Modern Latin America 1 course unit<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; PSC 230, 231 Government &amp; Politics of Europe 1 course unit<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; PSC 232 Government &amp; Politics of East Asia 1 course unit<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; PSC 234, 235 Government &amp; Politics of Russia 1 course unit<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; PSC 237 Government &amp; Politics of Africa 1 course unit<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; PSC 248, 249 Governments &amp; Politics of the Middle East 1 course unit</p>
<p><strong>Concentrations:</strong></p>
<p>Optimally, the student will follow one of the following four designated concentrations. However, where there is a compelling academic reason, such as education abroad, the student may make substitutions in any of the five concentrations or design a unique concentration (such as Developing Nations Studies) in consultation with his or her faculty advisor and the director of the major.</p>
