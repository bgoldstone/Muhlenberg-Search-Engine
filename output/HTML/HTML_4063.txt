  <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The</span><strong> MASTER'S IN APPLIED ANALYTICS (MAA) </strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">prepares graduates to go beyond data management and governance and develop the data analytics skills necessary to address various operational contexts.&nbsp;&nbsp;</span>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Through a grounding in statistical analysis and data applications, students learn to find critical patterns within datasets, create descriptive data summaries and visualizations, and effectively communicate results. Students refine their skills through team projects and a range of problem-solving contexts, from urban analytics, urban simulation and geodemographics to predictive analytics and population health, sustainability analytics, and epidemiology.</span></p>
<p><strong>Students complete the MAA in two years, taking two course units per semester in Fall, Spring and Summer terms for 12-course units.</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Class costs include books and materials; No hidden fees</li>
<li>Fixed tuition for the duration of your cohort</li>
<li><a href="/graduatestudies/graduateacademics/hybridlow-residencymodel/">Hybrid online format</a></li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Learn essential career skills</strong></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Demonstrate a solid understanding of database design, schemas, modeling, validation, cleansing, preparation, and end-user experience</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Demonstrate proficiency in the use of contemporary quantitative tools, analysis methods, data model design, and trend analysis&nbsp;</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Discriminate when to use specific tools and methods appropriately; and demonstrate the ability to plan, design, and create models for managerial decision making.</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Prepare, transform, and load large sets of data ("big data"); demonstrate the ability to summarize data sets into manageable aggregate datasets in actionable intelligence and key performance indicators.</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Show mastery of tools and ability to select appropriate predictive and stochastic modeling methods for effective decision-making and planning.</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Show mastery of contemporary tools, including programming and proprietary application languages, to develop and transform functional and informative data.</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Demonstrate knowledge of ethical treatment of data, including applying ethics and judgment to data analysis and interpretation; and regulatory requirements regarding privacy, confidentiality, and governance.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>
<p><strong><a href="/graduatestudies/appliedanalytics/coursescheduledescriptions/">Course descriptions for the Master&rsquo;s in Applied Analytics</a></strong></p>
<p><strong><a href="/graduatestudies/graduateadmission/requirements/">Admission&nbsp;Requirements</a></strong></p>
<p>Muhlenberg College admits applicants whose profile (a combination of prior academic performance, career accomplishments, community engagement through volunteerism, and other leadership activities) demonstrates the greatest potential for graduate study and contribution to their profession and a diverse society.&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<hr>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>The GRADUATE CERTIFICATE IN APPLIED ANALYTICS FOR ORGANIZATIONS</strong> is a six-course, one-year program where professionals develop the necessary data skills to identify patterns, analyze results, and predict future outcomes. All the courses in the certificate apply towards the masterâs degree, which can be completed in a second year.</p>
<p><strong>Career Opportunities</strong></p>
<p>Graduates who obtain a Certificate in Applied Analytics from Muhlenberg College will be able to take on contemporary data challenges in their work environment and prepare to pursue opportunities with a variety of employers, including, but not limited to, the following list of Lehigh Valley organizations:</p>
<ul>
<li>Deloitte LLP</li>
<li>Oracle Corporation</li>
<li>IQVIA</li>
<li>Air Products</li>
<li>Lehigh Valley Health Network</li>
<li>Johnson &amp; Johnson</li>
<li>Liquid Interactive</li>
<li>Comcast Corporation</li>
<li>Lukeâs Health Network</li>
<li>Vanguard Group Inc</li>
<li>JPMorgan and Chase &amp; Co</li>
<li>Bristol-Myers Squibb Company</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;<strong>The skills and knowledge you learn will enable you to:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Source, clean and transform data into descriptive summaries that can be presented, understood, and lead to action.</li>
<li>Apply foundational techniques and principles of computing and utilize mathematical and statistical models for decision making</li>
<li>Demonstrate ability to understand and explain database application design, data models, management, storage techniques, query languages, and database integration</li>
<li>Select and use appropriate tools for the analysis of data</li>
<li>Apply oral, visual and written communication skills to collaborate effectively with key stakeholders and influence decision-makers</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Courses:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>MAA 502 Statistical Tools for Analytics</li>
<li>MAA 504 Business Intelligence</li>
<li>MAA 506 Predictive Analytics</li>
<li>MAA 508 Data Warehouse and Mining</li>
<li>MAA 512 An Introduction to Programming Languages</li>
<li>MAA 514 Data Visualization</li>
</ul>
