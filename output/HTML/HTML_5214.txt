<h2>Living Writers Series Concludes with a reading by Chris Ware, Award-Winning Graphic Novelist </h2>

<img src="/media/contentassets/images/newscenter/ChrisWare-250x295.jpg" style="float:left; margin:8px 8px 8px 0" alt="News Image">

Muhlenberg Collegeâs Living Writers series will conclude with a reading by graphic novelist Chris Ware on November 18, 2013 at 7:00 p.m. in Miller Forum, Moyer Hall.

<p style="color:#4D4D4D;font-size:small;">




  



<span style="text-size:small;">	&nbsp;Friday, November 15, 2013  03:35 PM</span></p>

<!-- FULL WIDTH Image -->



<p>&zwnj;While attending the University of Texas at Austin,&nbsp;Ware&nbsp;published a regular comic strip in the student newspaper, which Art Spiegelman&nbsp;discovered. This led to Ware being published in <em>RAW</em> magazine.<span>&nbsp;</span></p>
<p>Since his graduation, Ware has been published in the pages of <em>New City</em> and <em>The Chicago Reader</em>. These works have formed the bulk of material that he's been collecting in his regular periodical, <em>The ACME Novelty Library</em> which reached its 20th issue in 2010.</p>
<p>From both this strip and the periodical, Ware wrote the graphic novel <em><span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">Jimmy Corrigan - the Smartest Kid on Earth</span></em><span style="color: black;">. This book </span>received an American Book Award, the Guardian First Book Award, and the French comics award "L'Alph Art."&nbsp; In 2009 <em>Jimmy Corrigan</em> was named as one of the "100 Best Books of the Decade" by <em>The Times</em> (London).&nbsp;</p>
<p>Ware is also the author of <em><span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">The Acme Novelty Datebook</span></em><span style="color: black;"> (</span>Volumes 1 and 2), <em style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">Drawn &amp; Quarterly</em> (2003 &amp; 2007), <em><span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">Quimby the Mouse</span></em>(Fantagraphics, 2003), and was the editor of the 13th issue of <em><span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">McSweeney's</span></em>(2005), and was the guest editor of Houghton-Mifflin's <em><span style="color: black; text-decoration: none; text-underline: none;">Best American Comics 2007</span></em><span style="color: black;">.</span> He is a contributor to <em>The New Yorker</em>, and was the cartoonist chosen to inaugurate the <em>New York Times Magazine</em>'s "Funny Pages" section in late 2005.</p>
<p>He is currently at work on a long-form graphic novel, <em>Rusty Brown</em>. Ware's work has appeared in many national and international art exhibits, including the Whitney Biennial exhibit. He continues to publish his strips as he finishes them in the pages of the <em>Virginia Quarterly Review. </em>He was recently awarded the Hoi Fellowship by the newly formed United States Artists, a non-profit organization that makes direct grants to working artists.</p>
<p>His novel, <em>Building Stories, </em>was named one of the New York Times "10 Best Books of 2012," and was awarded the Lynd Ward Prize for Graphic Novel of the Year.</p>
<p>Living Writers, established in 1994, is a course offered through Muhlenberg College's <a href="http://muhlenberg.edu/main/academics/english/">English department</a> once every three years that brings six established and emerging writers to Muhlenberg College. The students read their recent works and then interact with the authors directly as they visit campus for a day.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">The writers come to class, have lunch with students and then give a public reading of their work in the evening.</p>
