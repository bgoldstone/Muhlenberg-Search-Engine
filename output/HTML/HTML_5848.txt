  <p>Academic advising is integral to your development as a student and important for your educational, career and personal goals. Advisors are a part of your network at Muhlenberg and can help to guide you through the planning of your courses, connect you with on and off campus opportunities, resources and individuals who will be important to your development. Academic advisors are only one type of mentor for you at Muhlenberg. Other faculty and staff, coaches, alumni and peers may serve in an advisory capacity. Your academic advisor can help you to build your personal network. Advisors offer you advice as you move throughout your college experience. You are responsible for your own decisions on your educational journey.</p></div>

    <div class="panel-body">
        <p>Throughout your time as a student, you will engage with different academic advisors.</p>
<ul>
<li>Summer advisor: during the summer of your entering year your summer advisor will begin to discuss your academic and career interests and get to know you on a more personal level in order to help you select your first semester courses.</li>
<li>First Year advisor: all First Year students are assigned an academic advisor based upon your assignment to a First Year Seminar. All students enter Muhlenberg College without a declared major. You will remain with your FY advisor until you declare a major.</li>
<li>Major advisor: once you formally declare a major you will be transferred to a faculty member in the department or program of interest. If you are a double major you will have two advisors. Advisor assignment varies by department/program. In some instances you may be able to choose your advisor, in other instances you will be assigned based upon advisor workload. You must meet with your major advisor(s), at minimum, prior to registration for the upcoming semester.</li>
<li>Minor advisor: if you formally declare a minor you will also be assigned a minor advisor. While you are not required to meet with your minor advisor prior to registration, it is strongly recommended that you do so in order to ensure that you are making satisfactory progress towards the minor.</li>
<li>Professional/career advisor: if you plan to enter medical school or law school we also have pre-professional advisors who will meet with you to discuss your career plans and make recommendations about appropriate courses for your intended career.</li>
</ul>
