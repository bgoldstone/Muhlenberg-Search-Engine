<h2>MOL vs. MBA - Which Graduate Program is Right for YOU?</h2>





<p style="color:#4D4D4D;font-size:small;">
By:



  Vincent Arezzi
- Director of Marketing
- Division of Graduate and Continuing Education  



<span style="text-size:small;">	&nbsp;Thursday, October 15, 2020  03:41 PM</span></p>

<!-- FULL WIDTH Image -->
<img src="https://www.muhlenberg.edu//media/contentassets/images/gce/1200x675 Career Goals Achieved.png" style="width:100%;" alt="News Image"><br/><span style="color:#4D4D4D;font-size:small;font-style:italic;"></span><br/><br/>


<p>With a wide array of schools now offering an MBA combined with the many options for part-time and online programs, the MBA has become one of the most popular choices among graduate students. However, popularity does not guarantee a good fit for each applicant and is certainly no measure of a programâs alignment with your individual goals, aptitudes, and skills.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<h3><strong>Who should consider an MBA?</strong></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The Master of Business Administration(MBA) has become the pinnacle of business education.&nbsp; It covers a wide spectrum of business knowledge in the major functional areas. It extends lessons learned in a business-related undergraduate program in both depth and rigor. The interdisciplinary curriculum allows for a wide variety of career opportunities, with many recognizing those three letters as a reflection of overall business knowledge and acumen.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The MBA, despite its rigor and depth, is a âgeneralâ interdisciplinary business program covering the many functional areas of business administration such as operations, finance, accounting, marketing, management, ethics, and human resources.&nbsp; It provides a solid business foundation for those looking to run a business or dive deeper into finance or operations. It also works well to give entrepreneurs a holistic view of business functions to guide their start-up ventures.&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Most MBA programs tend to be heaviest on the finance side, going into detail on capital management, debt, equity, financial statements, financial ratios, and the associated accounting behind those concepts. The MBA can provide a high-quality foundation for studentsâ¦ If those students will use the wide range of business knowledge and financial acumen the MBA will provide.&nbsp; Students seeking to fill operational or financial positions would most benefit from an MBA.&nbsp; Likewise, those seeking General Manager positions, or desire to run their own business would also benefit.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Donât have an interest in digging deeper into finance? Operations? Accounting?Entrepreneurship? Then ask yourself if another graduate program would be better suited to your personal goals.</span></p>
<h3><strong>Who should consider a <a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/graduatestudies/organizationalleadership" target="_blank">Masterâs in Organizational Leadership(MOL)</a>?</strong></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Unlike the MBAâs general approach to the functional aspects of organizations. The <a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/graduatestudies/organizationalleadership" target="_blank">MOL </a>is designed with a specific focus on business leadership and management.&nbsp; The MOL integrates practical leadership applications with an academic study on leadership theory to build a complete set of skills needed to drive success within an organization. This program is designed specifically for the professional seeking upward mobility into management positions within any organization.&nbsp; Graduates will be career-ready leaders capable of driving initiatives, managing change, developing staff, and acting ethically to achieve organizational goals.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">With a&nbsp; focus on developing you as an individual, and providing the skills needed in any management career trajectory, the MOL stands out as a more personal development oriented graduate program.&nbsp; The MOL is perfect for students that have spent time in their specific functional area of business, already developing expertise but needing the management experience and skillsets to achieve upward mobility. &nbsp; The MOL will help students add a mix of management and leadership skills to any functional area they are already well-versed in.&nbsp; The MOL does not focus on specific domains or industry skills.&nbsp; It is not specific to any business structure or functional area.&nbsp; It provides a foundation for management success and essential skills to lead teams, initiatives, cross-functional projects, and achieve organizational objectives.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">When choosing a degree program, one of the key considerations should be the development of skills specific to your career path. &nbsp; While there is some overlap in skill development between the MBA and MOL, there are also distinct differences.</span></p>

<h2><strong>MOL vs MBA - Defining your goals is the key to choosing</strong></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Both an MBA and&nbsp;<a href="https://www.muhlenberg.edu/graduatestudies/organizationalleadership" target="_blank">MOL</a> will provide students with valuable skills to enhance their careers.&nbsp; The one that best suits you is a matter of addressing the key skills youâll use in future roles.&nbsp; If a wide range of knowledge in functional areas is needed, the MBA is the clear winner.&nbsp; However, most graduates would benefit from the focus and specialization on essential management skills that a Masterâs in Organizational Leadership provides.&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></p>
