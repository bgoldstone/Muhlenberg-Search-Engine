  <h2 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Femi J. Johnson</strong></h2>
<h3 style="text-align: center;"><strong><em>Femi and The Collection</em></strong></h3>
<p style="text-align: center;"><strong>June 14 â August 1, 2019</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><strong><img src="/media/contentassets/images/about/gallery/martinartgallery/femi abstract story-scapes-1-460x635.jpg" alt="" style="     " /><br></strong></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Ancient Presence</em>, Acrylic on Canvas, 40" x 30â</p>
<p><strong>Martin Art Gallery</strong> at <strong>Muhlenberg College </strong>is pleased to present <strong><em>Femi and The Collection</em></strong>. This exhibition features 20 works by prominent area artist <strong>Femi J. Johnson</strong>, alongside selections from the <strong>Martin Art </strong><strong>Galleryâs Permanent Collection.</strong>&nbsp;Works will be on public display in our main gallery, located in our Philip Johnson-Designed Dorothy and Dexter Baker Center for the Arts from June 14<sup>th</sup> through August 1, 2019.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Martin Art Gallery will host an opening reception in the Center for the Arts atrium on June 14<sup>th</sup>, from 4:45 â 6:15pm. This will be in conjunction with two other shows opening that night featuring new works by area artists Zach Kleemeyer and Emily Strong, also on view from June 14 â Aug 1.</p>
<p>Born in Manhattan, Femi J. Johnson grew up in nearby Easton PA, where he took an initial interest in graphite and charcoal drawing. His early interest in rendering led to a creative professional career in heavy industry as a Master Draftsman &amp; Designer.&nbsp; After completing thousands of drawings over decades of work as a draftsman, he began to reevaluate his creative trajectory.&nbsp; In implementing his return to the fine arts, he undertook a two-year directed studio program in art at Hudson Valley College in New York State.&nbsp; His engagement with contemporary and modernist art inspired his then emerging studio practice. Today his work is a combination of abstract, painterly, and figurative elements, interspersed with collage and other mixed media.&nbsp; Since 2012 Femi has lived and worked in the Lehigh Valley, where he works out of Expressions Studio and Gallery in downtown Allentown.</p>
<p>Femi sees his work as a sort of meditation on existence, akin to breathing. His practice is a spiritual one, and through the addition of layers and fragments, he catalogs his experiences in paint and collage. His combination of formal and self-education result in works that unite high and low aesthetic sensibilities. The work weâve chosen to highlight in this exhibition is part of an ongoing series that he calls <em>Abstract Story-Scapes</em>.&nbsp; Selected MAG collection works were chosen to draw formal, aesthetic and conceptual connections to the themes and experiences in Femiâs <em>Story-Scapes</em>.&nbsp;</p>
