  <p>1. What‚s the difference between an internship and a mentorship?</p>
<table style="width: 100%;">
<tbody>
<tr>
<td style="width: 20%; vertical-align: top;" scope="col">
<p><strong>Definition</strong></p>
</td>
<td style="width: 40%; vertical-align: top;" scope="col">Work experience undertaken for the purpose of applying knowledge from the classroom to a practical work environment and actively reflecting on that activity. This can also be an experience that allows a student to gain insights into a profession. See row below about research internships.</td>
<td style="width: 40%; vertical-align: top;" scope="col">Allows students to collaborate with a faculty member of choice in order to develop an original project. Similar to an independent study. This is meant to encourage independent research and original work.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="width: 20%; vertical-align: top;" scope="col">
<p><strong>Registration Requirement</strong></p>
</td>
<td style="width: 40%; vertical-align: top;" scope="col">Must submit an internship registration form to the Registrar within the internship registration deadline. It must be registered as a DNA credit (DNA 960).</td>
<td style="width: 40%; vertical-align: top;" scope="col">Must discuss a research plan/proposal with the faculty sponsor you want to work with before registering for the mentorship. Must submit a mentorship registration form (same as independent study registration form) to the Registrar within the registration deadline. It must be registered as a DNA credit (DNA 970).</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="width: 20%; vertical-align: top;" scope="col">
<p><strong>Research</strong></p>
</td>
<td style="width: 40%; vertical-align: top;" scope="col">Working as a research <strong>assistant</strong> to Faculty, you don‚t have authorship over the research. You are assisting the professor in their research.</td>
<td style="width: 40%; vertical-align: top;" scope="col">Doing original research <strong>under</strong> supervision of faculty, find faculty whose research is related to your original research to be your ‚mentor.‚</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="width: 20%; vertical-align: top;" scope="col">
<p><strong>Final Product</strong></p>
</td>
<td style="width: 40%; vertical-align: top;" scope="col">Final reflection paper and weekly or bi-weekly logs submitted to the faculty internship supervisor.</td>
<td style="width: 40%; vertical-align: top;" scope="col">It can take any form as long as it is agreeable to the student and the&nbsp;mentoring faculty member. It must beashareable form that reflects your work.
<p><strong>Science</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Typically done in a research lab setting</li>
<li>May be collaborating with others in the lab, but must have individual research assignments with definite outcomes</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Social Sciences/Humanities </strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Independent research study supervised by a full-time Berg faculty which typically leads to a research paper</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Arts</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Can produce a research paperCan culminate in an original artistic production that should have some scholarly narrative associated with it</li>
</ul>
</td>
</tr>

    <div class="panel-body">
        <p>A Dana Mentorship is similar to an independent study. Many Danas in the sciences do their Dana mentorship in a research lab setting and may be collaborating with others in the lab but they must have individual research assignments with definite outcomes. In social sciences and humanities, a Dana mentorship (DNA 970) typically is an independent research study supervised by a full-time Berg faculty which typically leads to a research paper. In the Arts, a Dana mentorship can also produce a research paper but can also culminate in an original artistic production that should have some scholarly narrative associated with it.</p>

If they cannot work with you, ask if they can recommend another professor to reach out to. You can reach out to local organizations or various Muhlenberg administrative offices for internships as well. For example, the <a href="/aboutus/careercenter/">Career Center</a> has a helpful ‚<a href="/media/contentassets/pdf/about/careercenter/student/Job Search Guide.pdf">Job & Internship Search</a>‚ guide.</p>

    <div class="panel-body">

Most Danas conduct research in their majors and minors and they take advantage of Muhlenberg faculty expertise. As previously mentioned, a mentorship is an independent research that is conducted under the supervision of a professor. If you or your prospective Dana mentor have questions, please arrange a meeting with Dr. Hashim.</p>

          7. What makes an internship or mentorship count as 0.5 or 1.0 credits?
  </p>

    <div class="panel-body">
        <ul>
<li>‚Independent Study/Research: Typically, no more than one course unit is awarded for each independent study/research course, and no fewer than <span style="text-decoration: underline;">9-12 hours of work per week</span> (Fall/Spring) or <span style="text-decoration: underline;">126-168 hours per semester</span> (Fall/Spring/Summer) are required for each course unit earned.‚ You may choose to pursue 0.5 credit unit mentorship (61-84 hours/semester). If you do so, you will need to pursue another 0.5 credit unit mentorship to meet the minumum Dana requirement of 1.0 credit of mentorship.</li>
<li><span style="text-decoration: underline;">61-84 hours</span> for 0.5 credit internship, <span style="text-decoration: underline;">126-168 total hour</span> for 1.0 credit internship

          8. Is it possible to get an internship/mentorship over the summer? Does doing an
internship/mentorship over the summer cost money?
  </p>

       aria-labelledby="summer-research">
    <div class="panel-body">
        <p>Yes, it is possible to get an internship or mentorship over the summer. Unfortunately, it does cost extra. The bill, depending on the Dana mentorship/internship work/course load is for 1.0 or 0.5 credits of summer courses. Because of the additional billing, Danas are strongly encouraged to complete their mentorship and internship within the regular academic year so that they do not incur additional expenses.</p>
<p>Danas may apply for summer research grants as an option for completing their mentorship. These grants are competitive, but they provide a stipend to the student, as well as free housing for 8 weeks of summer research. More information about these grants can be found on <a href="/academics/research/">Muhlenberg's Research and Scholarship website</a>.</p>
