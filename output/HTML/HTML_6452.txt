<td valign="top">
<p style="margin-top: 4px; margin-bottom: 0px; font-size: 80%;"><strong><a id="peck"></a>James Peck, Theatre Program Chair</strong></p>
<p style="margin-top: 4px; margin-bottom: 0px; font-size: 80%;">Professor: Directing, Performance Studies, and Theatre History<br>Ph.D., New York University; M.F.A., University of California, San Diego; B.A., Carleton College<br>Member, Stage Directors &amp; Choreographers Society</p>
<p style="font-size: 70%;">Dr. James Peck has directed over 60 productions of plays, operas, and musicals at theatres throughout the United States. He has published numerous scholarly articles in academic journals and in books from university presses. His current research focuses on the history and practice of directing. He is Series Editor of <em>The Great North American Stage Directors</em>, an eight-volume history of the art form of theatre direction published by Methuen Drama.&nbsp;He has also served as editor of<span>&nbsp;</span><em>Theatre Topics</em><span>&nbsp;</span>and book review editor of<span>&nbsp;</span><em>Theatre Journal</em>.</p>
<p style="font-size: 70%;">At Muhlenberg,&nbsp;Jim has received the Class of '32 Research Professorship, the Lindback Award for Distinguished Teaching, the Williams Award for Research by a Junior Faculty Member, and the First-Year Advising Award.&nbsp;<span>In 2020, he received the Oscar Brockett Award for the Outstanding Teacher of Theatre in Higher Education from The Association for Theatre in Higher Education (ATHE).&nbsp;Jim</span>&nbsp;chaired the Department of Theatre &amp; Dance from 2006 to 2013. In addition to Muhlenberg, he<span>&nbsp;has taught at the Yale School of Drama, the New York University Tisch School of the Arts, and the Playwrights Horizons Theater School.</span></p>
<p style="font-size: 80%; text-align: right;">he/him/his<strong>&nbsp; |&nbsp;&nbsp;<a href="mailto:jimpeck@muhlenberg.edu">jimpeck@muhlenberg.edu</a>&nbsp; |&nbsp; x3582</strong></p>
</td>
</tr>

<td align="center" valign="top"><img src="/media/contentassets/images/theatreampdance/images/theatrefaculty/lustig headshot w.jpg" alt="" style="     " /></td>
<td valign="top">
<p style="margin-top: 4px; margin-bottom: 0px; font-size: 80%;"><strong><a id="lustig"></a>Rebecca Lustig,&nbsp;</strong><strong><span>Director of the Co-Curricular/Production Program</span></strong></p>

<p style="font-size: 70%;">In addition to his more than 100 designs for Muhlenberg College Theatre and Muhlenberg Summer Music Theatre over the past 36 years, Curtis Dretsch‚s designs have been seen in New York City, Philadelphia, Washington, D.C., Baltimore, Dallas, Edinburgh, and London.
