      <p class="panel-title pull-left">
          How do I qualify for financial aid?
  </p>
      <p class="panel-title pull-right">
        
            <span class='fa fa-plus-square-o' ></span>
      </p>	
       


      <div class="clearfix"></div>
  	</div>

  <div id="12"

       role="tabpanel" 
       aria-labelledby="1">
    <div class="panel-body">
        <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Muhlenberg uses the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) as the trigger for federal and state entitlement aid, as well as for Muhlenbergâs institutional need-based grant aid. Students are also required to complete the Muhlenberg Scholarship and Aid application, which may be found in each studentâs financial aid portal. Muhlenbergâs deadlines for filing these forms are: Early Decision I â November 15; Early Decision II â February 1; Regular Decision â February 1.</span></p>
<p><strong>We strongly encourage families to utilize the IRS Data Retrieval Tool (DRT) when completing the FAFSA. If your family is eligible, using the DRT may eliminate the need for additional documentation. The FAFSA is found here: </strong><a href="https://studentaid.ed.gov/sa/fafsa"><strong>https://studentaid.ed.gov/sa/fafsa</strong></a><strong>&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Students should also complete the Muhlenberg College Scholarship &amp; Aid Application in late October</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;and before </span><strong>February 1, 2022. </strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">This form may only be completed after a student activates his or her Muhlenberg <a href="https://netp.muhlenberg.edu/NetPartnerStudent/Logon.aspx?ReturnUrl=%2fNetPartnerStudent%2fDefault.aspx">Financial Aid Online account</a>.</span></p>
<p><br><br></p>

      <p class="panel-title pull-left">
          What is the income cut-off for financial aid?
  </p>
      <p class="panel-title pull-right">
        
            <span class='fa fa-plus-square-o' ></span>
      </p>	
       


      <div class="clearfix"></div>
  	</div>
  </a>
  <div id="22" 
       class="panel-collapse collapse" 
       role="tabpanel" 
       aria-labelledby="2">
    <div class="panel-body">
        <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">There is no income cut-off for financial aid. The primary driver of the analysis of need is family income. However, both the FAFSA and our own institutional application will take into account assets, number in college, and the age of the oldest parent. Unreimbursed medical expenses and expenses for private secondary school education are some additional factors used by the Muhlenberg Scholarship and Aid application.</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

      <p class="panel-title pull-left">
          Will other family members in college affect my financial need?
  </p>
      <p class="panel-title pull-right">
        
            <span class='fa fa-plus-square-o' ></span>
      </p>	
       


      <div class="clearfix"></div>
  	</div>
  </a>
  <div id="42" 
       class="panel-collapse collapse" 
       role="tabpanel" 
       aria-labelledby="4">
    <div class="panel-body">
        <p><span>Muhlenberg College not only looks at who else (siblings only) in the family is enrolled in college, but also at the costs and financial aid involved at the other institution. We will divide the expected parent contribution accordingly. For example, if a sibling is enrolled at a community college, the parent contribution will not be divided in half as it would have been had that student been attending a school with a similar cost to Muhlenberg. Instead, it will be divided proportionally.</span></p>

    <div class="panel-body">
        <p>There are several loan options available to students and their parents.</p>
<p><strong>Federal Direct Subsidized Student Loan:</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>This is a need-based loan that goes in the student's name. It is subsidized by the federal government so there are no payments required as long as the student is enrolled in school on at least a half-time basis. First-year students may borrow $3,500, sophomores $4,500 and juniors and seniors $5,500. The interest rate changes each July 1st.<strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;For AY 21/22 loans it is fixed at 3.37%.</span></strong></p>
<p><strong>Federal Direct Unsubsidized Student Loan:</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>This is also a loan that goes in the student's name. Interest payments can be deferred until after graduation or are made while the student is in school. Beginning with the 2008-2009 academic year,<span>&nbsp;</span><span>all</span><span>&nbsp;</span>students are eligible for a $2,000 unsubsidized&nbsp;Direct Federal Loan.<span>&nbsp;</span><br><br>In addition, students that do not qualify for the maximum subsidized loan for their grade level may borrow the difference in the unsubsidized loan. Freshmen may borrow $3,500, sophomores $4,500, juniors and seniors $5,500<span>&nbsp;</span><strong>less any subsidized Student Loan they received.</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>The interest rate changes each July 1st. For AY&nbsp;21<strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">/22 loans it is fixed at 3.37%.</span></strong></p>
<p><strong>Federal Direct PLUS Loan:</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>This is a loan that the parent(s) of the student take out. The maximum amount that may be borrowed is the Cost of&nbsp;Attendance minus any other financial aid. Principal and interest payments or interest only payments are required and the interest changes each July 1st.</p>
<p><strong>Alternative Educational Loans:</strong><span>&nbsp;</span>These are loans that go in the studentâs name. Students normally have a&nbsp;cosigner for the loan. The maximum amount that may be borrowed is the cost of&nbsp;attendance minus any other financial aid. Principal<span>&nbsp;</span><span>and</span><span>&nbsp;</span>interest may be deferred while the student is enrolled in school. They have variable interest rates. Students may contact their lenders, credit card lenders or search the web for programs available.</p>
<p>Elm Select is a resource that can assist in searching for the right alternative loan for your particular situation.&nbsp;<a href="https://www.elmselect.com/v4/school/784/program-select" target="_blank">This link</a> will take you to their loan search engine.</p>
