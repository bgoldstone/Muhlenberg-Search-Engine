<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>City of Allentown Student Overlay District Information&nbsp;â</strong></span></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><strong>&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><strong>What is the Student Overlay District?</strong></p>
<p>In an attempt to protect the neighborhoods around&nbsp;<strong>Allentown Colleges</strong>, the City of Allentown zoning regulations were changed in 1997 and the&nbsp;<strong>Allentown Student Residence Overlay District</strong>&nbsp;was created. The overlay district was expanded in spring of 2018.<br> <br> The purpose of the Student Residence Overlay district is to limit the negative effects on single family homes around a college created by overcrowding, additional parking needs, and lack of maintenance on the structure and grounds. It is intended to keep the " residential character" of the neighborhood.<br> <br> This&nbsp;<strong>Allentown Zoning Regulation</strong>&nbsp;may have an effect on any property being considered as a rental for college students.&nbsp;</p>
<p align="center"><strong>&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p align="center"><strong>How does this zoning ordinance impact you?</strong></p>
<p>If the property is in the Student Residence Overlay District, it may only be rented out to 1 or 2 students max. Renting to 3 or more college students makes the property a Student Residence which is not allowed under the Student Residence Overlay District. If you are part of a group greater than 2 students this may impact you.</p>
<p align="center"><strong>Where is the Student Residence Overlay District?</strong></p>
<p>It is bounded on the north by Washington Street, on the west by North Broad Street, on the east by 19th and Leh streets and on the south by Parkway Boulevard. The district incorporates an L-shaped swath of properties in the 1900 block of Chew Street and the 200 block of N. 20th. Basically all the streets surrounding Muhleneberg for several blocks in every direction.</p>
<p>If you need assistance determining if a property is in the overlay please contact&nbsp;Housing &amp; Residence Life (484)-664-3180</p>
<p align="center"><strong>Are there exceptions to the ordinance?</strong></p>
<p>Yes. There is a list of properties that have âgrandfatheredâ status. These are the only properties in the overlay where more than 2 students can reside. However, only up to 4 students can reside in these properties. Here is a list of properties:</p>
