<h2>Muhlenberg Celebrates Student, Faculty and Staff Achievements at 2018 Honors Convocation</h2>



Chris Borick, professor of political science, delivered the convocation address: "Questioning Yourself: The Value of Self Reflection in a Polarized Era."

<p style="color:#4D4D4D;font-size:small;">




  



<span style="text-size:small;">	&nbsp;Monday, April 30, 2018  08:45 AM</span></p>

<!-- FULL WIDTH Image -->
<img src="https://www.muhlenberg.edu//media/contentassets/images/newscenter/academics/2018-HonorsConvocationBorick.jpg" style="width:100%;" alt="News Image"><br/><span style="color:#4D4D4D;font-size:small;font-style:italic;"></span><br/><br/>


<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The Muhlenberg College Honors Convocation is a formal academic ceremony designed to honor and celebrate leadership and scholarly excellence &amp; achievement. Held each April in advance of the formal Commencement ceremonies in May, this event serves to recognize select members of the faculty and student body for their contributions to Muhlenberg College, to their peers and to their disciplines.</span></p>
<p><strong><strong><br></strong></strong><strong>The Class of 1932 Research Professor<br></strong><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">The Class of 1932 Research Professorship provides an opportunity for a faculty member to&nbsp;</span></em><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">pursue a year of full-time research.</span></em></p>
<p>James Peck, Professor of Theatre and Dance</p>
<p><strong><strong>&nbsp;</strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>The Donald B. Hoffman Research Fellowship<br></strong><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">The Donald B. Hoffman Research Fellowship is named in honor of Mr. Donald B. Hoffman,&nbsp;</span></em><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">Class of 1932, and is given to encourage scholarship, research or creative activity which results&nbsp;</span></em><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">in publication, performance or exhibition.</span></em></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Macia Morgan, Associate Professor of Philosophy &amp; Director, Women‚s &amp; Gender&nbsp;</span>Studies</p>
<p><strong><strong>&nbsp;</strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>The Daniel J. and Carol Shiner Wilson Award<br></strong><em><span style="font-weight: 400;">The Daniel J. and Carol Shiner Wilson Award is named for Dr. Daniel Wilson, professor of history, and Dr. Carol Shiner Wilson, dean of the College for Academic Life and assistant professor of humanities, and is given to faculty members</span></em> <em><span style="font-weight: 400;">to assist in the completion of significant work of scholarship or comparable professional activity.</span></em></p>
