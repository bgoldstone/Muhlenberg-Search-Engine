  <p>For the most recent&nbsp;<strong><a href="https://docs.google.com/document/d/1bG7uQqXU68m0z3k2GNKxa_wtQHM0_oiXjG5OJJzjBcQ/edit" target="_blank">student international technology tips file</a></strong></p>
<p>For the most recent<strong>&nbsp;<a href="https://docs.google.com/document/d/14xcIDfiAslyhrlT5S5Sz4vc6x6_vegc-ZccqFLetpPc/edit" target="_blank">faculty/staff international technology and pedagogical tips file</a></strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Unable to access the files? The general content is also available below.</p>
<p><strong><a href="#For Students">For Students</a></strong></p>
<p><strong><a href="#For Faculty/Staff">For Faculty/Staff</a></strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong><a id="For Students"></a>For students learning remotely in international locations&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p><strong>Canvas</strong></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">New to Muhlenberg and/or to Canvas? Please access this </span><strong><a href="https://muhlenbergcollege.hosted.panopto.com/Panopto/Pages/Viewer.aspx?id=2614ef31-f6d2-4abe-bf2c-acea003e3946" target="_blank">Canvas orientation video</a></strong></li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Time zone considerations</strong></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>Time zones</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"> are something to consider early in conversations with faculty and in determining your schedule for academic work</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">If in different time zones, make sure to check the settings on your online materials, as some tools have the ability to adjust to the local/regional time, while others will show the Eastern time zone of Muhlenberg regardless of where you are currently</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Have conversations with faculty related to recording sessions if time zone differences result in a web session in the middle of the night based on time zones</span></li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Communications</strong></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>Be in contact with faculty and staff at Muhlenberg </strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">should there be any changes or questions/concerns arising at any time in the semester and your studies related to technologies and course material access</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Contact your faculty so they know </span><strong>you are receiving messages and also with an additional means of communication</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (such as a personal email address) should you be comfortable doing so</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you are </span><strong>noticing any content within Canvas or other areas of your course are not opening or seem to have error messages</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"> that your peers are not mentioning, let your faculty know</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Best efforts are being made, however, sites or web addresses might restrict over geography or not be available in all regions</span></li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Items to check</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Work with faculty on adapting any alterations to technologies</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"> to be used in a course if know there is an issue and do so at the start of the semester (or as soon as aware of any issue)</span></li>
<li><span style="font-weight: 400;">Check any </span><strong>eBooks</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"> in your courses early to ensure access to content and let faculty know if there are any items not working</span></li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Collaboration and Community</strong></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Leverage ways to connect with your classmates through technology, whether in discussion boards or other areas your faculty made available, Muhlenberg related activities and events being broadcast when possible and of interest, and/or by forming virtual study or conversation groups. You are very much part of the Muhlenberg community</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Technical assistance</strong></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>If you are experiencing technical issues</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">, please let the Office of Information Technology know as soon as possible, so that we can assist you.</span></li>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Understanding there may be a time difference, please provide the best time to contact you if follow-up is needed.&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></li>
</ul>
</ul>
<ul>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">To </span><strong>initiate a support request online:</strong></li>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Visit </span><a href="https://servicedesk.muhlenberg.edu/HEAT/"><strong>servicedesk.muhlenberg.edu/HEAT</strong></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (portal login required)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Click on the Service Catalog</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Report an Issue</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Once an incident has been created, you may check the status by clicking on "My Items"&nbsp;</span></li>
</ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>Want to drop us a note or suggestion, or perhaps you are unable to login? </strong><strong><a href="https://servicedesk.muhlenberg.edu/HEAT/alp.aspx?Scope=SelfService&amp;CommandId=NewServiceRequestByOfferingId&amp;Tab=ServiceCatalog&amp;Template=C316D5F94FCD4BDAB713980454CBAC47">Report an Issue (no login required)</a></strong></li>
</ul>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">Email </span><a href="mailto:helpdesk@muhlenberg.edu">helpdesk@muhlenberg.edu</a></strong></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-weight: 400;">Call 484-664-3566</span></li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong><strong>How can I access College resources from off campus? </strong><strong><br></strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">VPNs, or Virtual Private Networks, allow users to securely access a private network and share data remotely through public networks. If students require access to the campus network, they may contact the Student Help Desk for more information at helpdesk@muhlenberg.edu or 484-664-3566. Please note: VPN access does not guarantee access to campus resources like Google in countries which restrict their usage, specifically mainland China.</span></strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p><a id="For Faculty/Staff"></a><strong>For faculty/staff working with students who are&nbsp;remote learning outside United States</strong></p>
<p><strong>Flexible design</strong></p>
<ul>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>Consider flexibility in tools/technologies </strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">students need to complete tasks, providing options (</span><strong>multimodal</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Know that </span><strong>videos embedded in Canvas might come across country-specific restrictions (example: mainland China) if the videos originate from sites like YouTube</strong></li>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Muhlenberg-hosted videos might serve as an alternative, as will finding several video options on a topic or theme. Vimeo may be an alternative in some geographic regions, but not all.</span></li>
</ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>Check early with eBook providers</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"> if using digital books if there are workarounds for students outside United States if problems encountered, or of known issues</span></li>
</ul>
</ul>
<p><strong><strong>Plan adaptively for webconference/Zoom meeting</strong></strong></p>
<ul>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Consider whether having multiple video feeds (such as all students with webcams on) is needed as that can affect performance quality of video</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Consider providing the slides sharing in additional area (such as within Canvas) for any students to refer to outside of the meeting in case of bandwidth or performance issues</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Be mindful of time zones and weigh synchronous sessions (and whether to record) accordingly</span></li>
</ul>
</ul>
<p><strong>Look into the technology and tools used in your course, and any country-specific restrictions, limitations, or access issues, including ones such as</strong></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Information about<strong> <a href="https://support.zoom.us/hc/en-us/articles/203806119-Will-Zoom-Work-Internationally">Zoom</a></strong></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Information about </span><strong><a href="https://community.canvaslms.com/docs/DOC-11005-67952736995">Canvas</a></strong></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Information about </span><strong><a href="https://support.google.com/a/answer/2891389?hl=en">GSuite</a></strong></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>More tips </strong><span style="font-weight: 400;">are available in</span><strong><a href="https://knight.as.cornell.edu/guidance-faculty-getting-staying-connected-intl-students"> this document from Cornell University</a></strong></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>More information from Office of International Student Support</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"> can be found here: </span><strong><a href="https://docs.google.com/document/d/1Pu03vDcEP583nRw0QSKmz66dqstIF3lzatHKOB3Ll7c/edit?usp=sharing">‚Fall 2020 Guide for Faculty and Staff of F-1 International Students‚</a></strong></li>
</ul>
</ul>
<p><strong>Communications</strong></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>Facilitate upfront conversation setting expectations of how, when communicating with students and what to do if you are not receiving responses</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"> or expected messaging (lets you know if messages received, any challenges being encountered).&nbsp;</span></li>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Consider having the students send an email, with you sending a response early</span><strong> to ascertain if any issues with email access</strong></li>
</ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>Consider starting your course with the access conversation and a low/no stakes walkthrough</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"> where students submit something to you demonstrating things are working, or submitting a write up of the issue(s) encountered</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>Create a common ground area</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"> for conversation among students who are in person and those who are at a distance (ex. Virtual coffee/conversation forum in discussion board, collaboration space outside the class times that allows students to study/interact together via technology)</span></li>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">This provides more ways for community to form and reduces possible feelings of separation</span></li>
</ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>Connect students at a distance</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"> with most responsive routes for support for technology they will be using</span></li>
</ul>
