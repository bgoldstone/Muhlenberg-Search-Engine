  <p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-size: x-large;"><strong>Personal Protective Equipment Policy <br /> </strong></span><strong>Muhlenberg College<br /> Allentown, PA <br /> September 2005</strong></p>
<p align="left"><strong>Policy</strong><br /> Personal protective equipment including those for eyes, ears, face, head, and extremities, protective clothing, respiratory devices, protective shields and barriers shall be provided, utilized and maintained in a sanitary and reliable condition whenever deemed necessary by reason of hazards, processes or environment.</p>
<p align="left"><strong>Scope</strong><br /> This policy applies to all employees who by nature of their job function have the potential to be exposed or come into contact with audio,chemical, physical, radiological or biological hazards which by this exposure can cause illness, injury or impairment in the function of any part of the body.</p>
<p align="left">With the approval of the College Safety Committee, a department may propagate their own P.P.E. policy as long as it is in compliance with current OSHA regulations and requirements. Such approved policies must be submitted and attached to the College general policy for reference.</p>
<p align="left"><strong>Authority and Responsibility</strong></p>
<p align="left">Immediate Supervisors are responsible for:<br /><br /></p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>Ensuring personal protective equipment is available and providing personal protective equipment as required or upon request to all employees</li>
<li>Ensuring personal protective equipment is being used by each affected employee during all job tasks which require such protection.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<p align="left">Plant Operations is responsible for:<br /><br /></p>
<ul>
<li>Assessing the workplace to determine if hazards are present, or are likely to be present, which necessitate the use of personal protective equipment.</li>
<li>Communicating selection decisions to each affected employee</li>
<li>Selecting personal protective equipment that properly fits each affected employee.</li>
<li>Documenting aforementioned hazard assessment components utilizing a Personal Protective Equipment Assessment</li>
</ul>
<p align="left">Employees are responsible for:<br /><br /></p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>Wearing personal protective equipment upon the direction of their immediate supervisor.</li>
<li>Participating in training.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p><strong>Considerations</strong><br />Personal protective equipment devices alone shall not be relied on to provide protection against hazards, but shall be used in conjunction with guards, engineering controls, administration controls and sound manufacturing practices.</p>
<p align="left">When selecting personal protective equipment, utilize the following considerations as a basic directive.</p>
<div><ol>
<li>Application: What part of the body is being protected?</li>
<li>Chemical resistance: Will material maintain its structural integrity and protective qualities?</li>
<li>Strength: Is the material resistant to punctures, tears, and abrasions?</li>
<li>Flexibility: Do gloves provide the necessary flexibility?</li>
<li>Thermal Limits: Does clothing maintain its mobility and protective capacity in temperature extremes?</li>
<li>Cleanable: Can material be easily cleaned and reused?</li>
<li>Longevity: Will clothing resist aging?</li>
</ol></div>
<p align="left">Contact Plant Operations for personal protective equipment product recommendations.</p>
<p><strong>Hand Protection </strong></p>
<p align="left">Hand protection shall be worn when hands are exposed to hazards such as those from skin absorption of harmful substances, severe cuts or lacerations, severe abrasions, punctures, chemical burns, thermal burns and harmful temperature extremes.</p>
<p align="left">The type of hand protection used shall be based on the performance characteristics of the hand protection relative to the task(s) to be performed, conditions present, duration of use, and the hazards and potential hazards identified.</p>
<p align="left">With respect to the selection of gloves for protection against chemical hazards:<br /><br /></p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>The toxic properties of the chemical(s) must be determined; in particular, the ability of the chemical to cause local effects on the skin and/or to pass through the skin and cause systemic effects.</li>
<li>Generally, any &ldquo;chemical resistant&rdquo; glove can be used for dry powders.</li>
<li>For mixtures and formulated products (unless specific test data is available), a glove shall be selected on the basis of the chemical component with the shortest breakthrough time, since it is possible for solvents to carry active ingredients through polymeric materials.</li>
<li>Employees shall be able to remove the gloves in such a manner as to prevent skin contamination.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<p align="left"><strong>&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p align="left"><strong>Head Protection</strong></p>
<p align="left">Head protection shall be worn in areas where there is a potential for injury to the head from impact, flying or falling objects (e.g., working below other workers who are using tools and materials which could fall through grates), or electrical shock and burns.</p>
<p align="left">Helmets for protection against impact and penetration of falling objects shall comply with the &ldquo;American national Standard for Personal protection &ndash; protective Headwear for Industrial Workers Requirements&rdquo; (ANSI) Z89.1. Helmets for protection against electrical shock and burns shall comply with (ANSI) Z89.2-1971.</p>

<p align="left">The maximum permissible level for impact or impulse noise shall be 140 dB as measured with a sound level meter capable of indicating an instantaneous peak noise level. Impact and impulse noise are peaks or maxim of sound level, above the continuous background level, which have separation intervals greater than one sound. If peaks are done one second or less apart, the noise shall be considered continuous.</p>
<p align="left">Employees whose noise exposures equal or exceed the action level shall be provided with the necessary hearing protection, and shall comply with the rules and procedures.</p>
<p><strong>Eye/Face Protection</strong></p>
<p align="left">Suitable eye protection or face protection shall be worn when there is the potential for exposure to the eyes or face from flying particles, molten metal, liquid chemicals, acid or caustic liquids, chemical gases or vapors or potentially injurious light radiation. Side protection is required when there is a hazard potential from flying objects. Detachable side protectors (e.g., clip-on or slide-on shields) meeting the pertinent requirements are acceptable.</p>
<p align="left">Eye protection shall be durable, comfortable and easy to clean. Persons who vision requires the use of corrective lens and who by nature of their job duties require eye protection shall wear goggles or a full face shield that can be worn over the prescription lenses.</p>
<p align="left">There are four general classes of eye and face protection: safety glasses, face shields, goggles and welding helmets. The type of protection required shall be determined by the type and degree of the hazard and shall comply with ANSI Z87.1-1989 &ldquo;American National Standard Practice for Occupational and Educational Eye and Face Protection&rdquo;.</p>
<p align="left">Safety glasses shall be worn at all times in the following locations:<br /><br /></p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>Academic and research laboratories</li>
<li>Facilities Maintenance shops (e.g., welding, carpentry, automotive)</li>
<li>All areas where airborne materials are present</li>
<li>Clinics where invasive patient related tasks are conducted</li>
</ul>
</div>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p><strong>Foot Protection</strong></p>
<p align="left">Foot protection shall be worn when there is the potential for injury to the feet from falling or rolling objects, objects piercing the sole of the foot, electrical hazards, hot surfaces and slippery surfaces.</p>
<p align="left">Foot protection shall comply with ANSI Z-1991 &ldquo;American national Standard for Personal protection &ndash; Protective Footwear&rdquo;.</p>
<p align="left"><strong>Respirators</strong></p>
<p align="left">Use of respirators shall be done in accordance with the Respiratory Protection policy.</p>
<p><strong>Body Protection </strong></p>
<p align="left">Full body protection shall be worn when there is a potential for contamination or exposure to other parts of the body (e.g., legs, arms, back, chest) from heat, splashes from hot metals and liquids, impacts, cuts, chemicals and radiation.</p>
<p align="left">Body protection includes the following:<br /><br /></p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>Lab coats</li>
<li>Boot covers</li>
<li>Aprons</li>
<li>Bouffant caps</li>
<li>Tyvek suits</li>
<li>Coveralls</li>
</ul>
</div>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p><strong>Electrical Protective Devices</strong></p>
<p align="left">Rubber insulating equipment shall be used/worn to protect employees from shocks/burns while working on &ldquo;live&rdquo; electrical systems.</p>
<p align="left">Rubber insulating equipment shall comply with the following American Society for testing and materials (ASTM) standards:<br /><br /></p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>Specification for Rubber Insulating Gloves (D120-87)</li>
<li>Specification for Rubber Insulating Matting (ASTM D178-93 or D 178-88)</li>
<li>Specification for Rubber Insulating Blankets (ASTM D1048-93 or D 1048-88a)</li>
<li>Specification for Rubber Insulating Covers (ASTM D 1049-93 or D 1049-88</li>
<li>Specification for Rubber Insulating Line Hose (ASTM D 1050-90)</li>
<li>Specification for Rubber Insulating Sleeves (ASTM D 1051-87)</li>
</ul>
</div>
<p align="left">All electrical protective equipment shall be subject to periodic electrical tests conducted in accordance with appropriate voltages identified by ASTM standards to reliably indicate whether the insulating equipment can withstand the voltage involved. Insulating equipment failing to pass inspections or electrical tests shall NOT be used by employees.</p>
<p align="left">Rubber insulating equipment test intervals shall occur as follows:<br /><br /></p>
<ul>
<li>Rubber insulating line hoses shall be tested upon indication that the insulating valve is suspect.</li>
<li>Rubber insulating covers shall be tested upon indication that the insulating valve is suspect.</li>
<li>Rubber insulating blankets shall be tested before first issue and every twelve months thereafter.</li>
<li>Rubber insulating gloves shall be tested before first issue and every six months thereafter.</li>
<li>Rubber insulating sleeves shall be tested before first issue and every twelve months thereafter.</li>
</ul>
<p align="left"><em>Note:</em> If the insulating equipment has been electrically tested but not issued for service, it shall not be placed into service unless it has been electrically tested within the previous twelve months.</p>
<p align="left">All departments using rubber insulating equipment shall make the appropriate arrangements for testing of such equipment.</p>
<p><strong>Maintenance Schedules </strong></p>
<p align="left">Personal protective equipment shall be inspected, cleaned and maintained at regular intervals so that the personal protective equipment can be discarded, changed and/or decontaminated as deemed necessary. At a minimum, all personal protective equipment shall be discarded when it has become contaminated, worn, torn or has other integrity problems.</p>
<p align="left">Personal protective equipment provides the requite protection. It is important to ensure that contaminated personal protective equipment, which cannot be decontaminated, is disposed in a manner that protects employees from exposure to hazards.</p>
<p align="left"><em>Note:</em> Inspect personal protective equipment before each use for tears, punctures, holes, cuts, cracks, embedded foreign objects and texture changes (e.g., swelling, softening, hardening, becoming sticky or inelastic).</p>
<p><strong>Training</strong></p>
<h4 align="LEFT">&nbsp;</h4>
<h4 align="LEFT">Initial Training</h4>
<p align="left">Plant Operations or the appropriate department shall provide initial training for each employee who is required to use personal protective equipment. Each employee shall be trained in at least the following:<br /><br /></p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>When personal protective equipment is necessary.</li>
<li>What personal protective equipment is necessary.</li>
<li>How to properly don, doff, adjust, and wear personal protective equipment.</li>
<li>The limitations of the personal protective equipment.</li>
<li>The proper care, maintenance, useful life and disposal of the personal protective equipment.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<p align="left">Each affected employee shall demonstrate an understanding of the aforementioned training and the ability to use personal protective equipment properly before being allowed to perform work requiring the use of personal protective equipment.</p>
<h4 align="LEFT">&nbsp;</h4>
<h4 align="LEFT">Retraining</h4>
<p align="left">When there is reason to believe that any affected employee who has already been trained does not have the understanding and skill as required above, Plant operations or the affected department shall retrain each such employee. Circumstances where retraining is required include, but are not limited to, situations where:<br /><br /></p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>Changes in the workplace render previous training obsolete.</li>
<li>Changes in the types of personal protective equipment to be used render previous training obsolete</li>
<li>Inadequacies in an affected employee&rsquo;s knowledge or use of assigned personal protective equipment indicate that the employee has not retained the requisite understanding or skill.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<p align="left"><em><strong>Recordkeeping:</strong></em></p>
<p align="left">Plant Operations shall verify that each affected employee has received and understood the required training through a written certification containing the name of each employee trained, the date(s) of training and the subject of the certification.</p>
<p align="left">&nbsp;</p></div>
