<div class="fs-cell fs-lg-8">


  <p>In a wâorld that is becoming increasingly&nbsp;interdependent, study abroad represents a significant means by which students may better prepare themselves to face challenges of the future.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><strong>56 percent of Muhlenberg graduates from the Class of 2019 studied abroad. Between 250 and 300 students study abroad each year.<br></strong></p>
<p style="text-align: left;">Muhlenberg College encourages students in all science, social science, arts and humanities majors to study overseas at quality institutions in Europe, Australia, Asia, Latin America and Africa. Muhlenberg supports four discipline-specific study abroad opportunities: The London <a href="/main/academics/theatre-dance/">Theatre</a> Program at Goldsmiths College, University of London, and the program for <a href="/academics/abe/">Accounting, Business and Economics</a> majors at the University of Maastricht in the Netherlands are offered during the fall semester. A program for <a href="/academics/mediacom/">Media &amp; Communication</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a href="/academics/film-studies/">Film Studies</a> majors in Dublin, Ireland, and a <a href="/main/academics/theatre-dance/">Dance</a> program in Arezzo, Italy, are both offered during the spring semester. In addition, Muhlenberg has established exchange opportunities in Europe and in Japan.</p></div>
