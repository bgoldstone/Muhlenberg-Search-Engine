  <h3 class="p1">Applying to Professional School</h3>
<p class="p1">Our philosophy is that you should apply to professional school when your application is the strongest it can be. You should have a solid&nbsp;academic record (especially in the sciences), high standardized test scores, and lots of exposure to the field you are pursuing. You should also be engaging in research, developing leadership skills, and demonstrating your commitment to service.&nbsp; All of this takes time; therefore, your timeline will be unique to you and we are here to help with your planning.</p>
<p class="p1">Your success is our goal!&nbsp;</p></div>

        <p style="text-align: center;"><strong>HEALTH PROFESSIONAL SCHOOL&nbsp;<br></strong><strong>APPLICANT&nbsp;TIMELINE</strong></p>
<h4>DECEMBER</h4>
<ol>
<li>If you have not yet taken your entrance exam (MCAT, DAT, GRE, OAT), decide when you will do so and develop a study plan. You should typically allow yourself three months to prepare, but the timeline will also depend on your individual study schedule. Practice tests are crucial (under test conditions!), hand-in-hand with content review.&nbsp;</li>
<li><span style="font-weight: 400;">Begin work on the </span>Candidate Information Google Doc<strong>&nbsp;</strong>and<span style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;draft your essay.&nbsp;</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br></span></li>
<li><span style="font-weight: 400;">Investigate professional school admissions criteria and formulate the list of schools to which you will apply.&nbsp; The <strong><a href="https://students-residents.aamc.org/applying-medical-school/applying-medical-school-process/medical-school-admission-requirements/">MSAR</a></strong>, <strong><a href="https://choosedo.org/choose-do-explorer-registration/">Choose DO Explorer</a></strong>, </span><strong><a href="https://dentalschoolexplorer.adea.org/index">ADEA Dental School Explorer</a></strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>,</strong> and&nbsp;<strong><a href="https://applytovetschool.org/">VMSAR</a></strong> all provide information on choosing professional schools.&nbsp;<br></span></li>
<li>Consider who you will ask for faculty evaluations and letters of recommendation for your committee letter.</li>
<li>Some test prep materials may be available from the Health Professions Advising office.</li>
<li>If you will be abroad in the spring â speak with CailÃ­n about your interview before you leave.</li>
</ol>
<h4><strong>&nbsp;JANUARY</strong></h4>
<ol>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Request evaluations from Muhlenberg faculty for your committee letter.&nbsp; A maximum of four evaluations should be requested with at least two from faculty in the sciences.&nbsp;<br></span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Request 1-2 additional letters of recommendation (for example, a doctor whom you showed, an internship supervisor, or your coach/mentor.) These letters are optional but strongly encouraged (although some programs </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">require</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> a letter from a health professional.)&nbsp; If you will be applying to osteopathic medical school, you may need a letter from a DO.&nbsp;<br></span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Complete the</span> Candidate Information Google Doc.<span style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"><br></span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Submit your Request for Committee Letter.<strong><br></strong></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;">Schedule an interview with CailÃ­n<span style="font-weight: 400;">.&nbsp;Interview times will be available during February, March and April and are scheduled for an hour.&nbsp; Bring any questions you have to this interview.&nbsp; Be prepared to discuss your essay, extracurricular activities, and health related experiences.</span></li>
</ol>
